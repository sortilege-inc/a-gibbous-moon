A Gibbous Moon Over Constantinople

In the year of our Lord, the party gathered at the Calvert family farmhouse at Thymbra Farm, north of Constantinople, for a dinner with notable guests. Frederick and Frank Calvert hosted the gathering, where discussions of local archaeological excavations flourished amidst a mélange of French, Turkish, and English conversations. Osman Hamdi Bey, a prominent figure in the archaeological community, publicly praised Sidra al-Najjar and Ahmet for their contributions to the dig.

As the evening progressed, the atmosphere shifted to quieter exchanges and private dealings. Rufus Dimitrious, presented as a coastal guide, observed the wealthy guests while engaging in discreet transactions. Jean-Zera Mongé inquired about the dig's findings, and the evening culminated in dessert, where Gizem Faruki's baklava received accolades from the attendees. The night transitioned into the early morning, where Gizem practiced Sufi turning on the terrace, imparting her knowledge to younger girls, while Sidra wrestled with her curiosity about the dervishes in relation to her own jinn-touched magic. A sudden desiccation phenomenon struck, leaching color from a nearby flowering tree and causing an unsettling ringing in the ears of those present.

The following day, the group ventured to a nearby dig site where a local boy, Ahmet Yusuf, discovered a tree and a 30-foot radius of ground devoid of color. Inside the farmhouse, Jean-Zera detected faint magical residue, while Rufus persuaded a tipsy General to touch the leached tree, which yielded no immediate effects. The party learned of Heinrich Schliemann's mysterious disappearance, and Osman Hamdi Bey's frantic writing in a ciphered journal hinted at deeper troubles. Ahmet scouted ahead toward the dig area, obscuring traces of their passage, while Rufus relayed overheard whispers of "a hill and a key."

In a daring move, Jean-Zera and Rufus infiltrated a locked study, uncovering research focused on Troy and a hidden parchment titled The Lion’s Key. They also discovered a narrow crevice leading into a cave, which they explored, finding pre-Greek frescoes and a central hearth within the ruins. Their exploration took a perilous turn when a massive serpent emerged, demanding the removal of interlopers in the next chamber. The party learned that Schliemann and Calvert were trapped within a luminous ward, and after a tense standoff, they managed to disrupt the ward and copy the discovered spell.

Upon returning to the farmhouse, Heinrich Schliemann loudly proclaimed his discovery of ancient Troy, prompting Osman Hamdi Bey to remind everyone of the legal ramifications of exporting artifacts. An urgent summons required Osman to return to Constantinople that evening, and he requested Jean-Zera, Rufus, Ahmet, and Sidra as assistants. The group boarded a non-government steamer, where they encountered a Shade attack, which Sidra vanquished. However, they soon discovered that Osman Hamdi Bey's journal had been stolen, and a teleport ritual aboard the ship caused a hull breach, leading to a crisis as the vessel began to sink.

After the ship's emergency was addressed, the party arrived in Constantinople, where they were welcomed into Osman's home. They explored his extensive libraries and artifact rooms, where Sidra identified magical tomes while Ahmet covertly took a Turkish volume on djinn influence. A shared strange dream hinted at deeper magical influences, and Osman deputized the party to recover his journal, providing initial contacts for their task.

The party pursued leads in the Kasimpasha District, meeting Ahra 'the Wolf,' who offered assistance in recovering the journal in exchange for infiltrating criminal operations. They encountered an albasti, a winged creature preying on a sleeping mark, which they subdued after a fierce battle. Following this, they researched cursed sleep cases and traveled to the Undercity, where they met Kore at the Arraf Tavern and received an Undercity coin pass.

As they continued their investigations, the party split for the evening, with Gizem and Jean-Zera attending a clandestine gambling club while Sidra and Ahmet investigated disturbances at a cemetery. The gambling club led to an abduction attempt, which Gizem and Jean-Zera thwarted, returning to the game with a significant profit. Meanwhile, Sidra and Ahmet encountered scavengers at the cemetery, trading treats and discovering a wine cache before confronting an invisible ape-like creature.

After delivering the creature's body to Hano 'the Widow,' the party received rewards and secured permanent-entry coins for the Undercity. They prepared for the upcoming Festival of Candles, where they would face further challenges. The festival brought chaos as animated shadows stole Chique Manu's ring, leading to a rooftop confrontation and the arrival of the Ghost of March, who executed a perfumer in a public display of power.

In the aftermath, the party regrouped and planned their next steps, investigating the Ghost of March's actions and the implications of his connection to the Sekban uniforms. They traveled to the Kasimpasha District, where Ahra 'the Wolf' confirmed the journal recovery and assigned them to deal with unquiet dead beneath Saint Benoit as final payment. The party researched the church's history and entered through a graveyard, encountering animated cadavers and a ghost who tasked them with breaking seals and slaying his bound brother, Vlad.

Tensions escalated as they confronted Hayriah, a tomb-raider leader, and a vampire emerged, killing Hayriah and two of her escorts before retreating. The party pursued and defeated the vampire, recovering valuable artifacts and weapons. After a long rest, they exited the ruins and negotiated the sale of their findings, including a potion of necrotic resistance, before arranging a meeting with the Ghost of March.

As the party prepared for their next mission, they experienced interconnected dreams involving a sigil, which led them to investigate Osman Hamdi Bey's house. They discovered selective looting and recovered Osman's genuine journal, which contained a letter bequeathing his manor and detailing a diviner's location. The party debated potential allies and pursued leads at the Spectral Hippodrome, gathering information about the Crown of Sargon and The Shahmeran bound beneath the Basilica Cistern.

Their journey culminated in a confrontation with the Egyptian Kavalan, who executed a would-be assassin at Topkapi Palace. The party's investigation revealed memories of Jean-Zera, leading them to strategize their next steps. They infiltrated the Basilica Cistern, encountering marrows and a Medusa, before descending into a lower prison complex where they freed a sorceress.
