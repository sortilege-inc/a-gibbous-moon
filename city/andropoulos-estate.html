Andropoulos Estate

The Andropoulos Estate is known for its opulent display of curiosities and artifacts, often bordering on legality. It features intricately designed structures and rooms filled with marble statues and other valuable items, reflecting the wealth and influence of its owner, Giannis the Cut. The estate serves as a hub for dealings involving rare treasures and clandestine exchanges, attracting those navigating the underbelly of the city.

Following the Festival of Candles, the estate becomes a focal point for discussions about the Ghost of March and the political unrest surrounding the assassination of Nazif. Jean-Zera Mongé visits to consult with Giannis, who discusses rival treasure seekers, notably a competitor named Hayriah. Giannis proposes a 25% cut for Jean-Zera’s crew if they can outpace Hayriah in recovering treasures. The atmosphere combines banter and business, with Giannis showcasing legal curios and offering marble statues for sale. Jean-Zera also shares information about the festival, including an incident involving shadows attempting to steal a ring from Chique Manu, which elicits a reaction from Giannis. The estate is significant for facilitating connections and transactions among those involved in the city’s hidden dealings.
