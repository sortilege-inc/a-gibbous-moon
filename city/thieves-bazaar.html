Thieves Bazaar

The Thieves Bazaar is a bustling underground marketplace located within the Undercity, accessible through Nazif’s Scents in the Grand Bazaar. This vibrant area is populated by tieflings, half-orcs, half-elves, humans, and other magically altered beings, creating a melting pot of cultures and secrets. The bazaar features narrow pathways winding through various stalls and shops, with stone walls and carpets adorning the floors, crafting a warm yet shadowy atmosphere. Dimly lit, it is alive with the sounds of vendors hawking their wares and the movement of its eclectic inhabitants, making it a hub for trade and clandestine dealings.

Among the notable establishments within the Thieves Bazaar is the Arraf Tavern, a refuge for those who feel marginalized or cursed. Rumors circulate among the locals of a ghost known as the Ghost of March, adding an air of mystery to the tavern's already intriguing ambiance. The bazaar is also a focal point for those seeking passage to meet Kore, a significant figure within the area, and gather information about supernatural happenings linked to their adventures.

The market is renowned for its array of goods, including potions, poisons, and magical items. Notable items for sale include healing potions, a Knock Spell Scroll, and Boots of Elvenkind. It serves as a hub for shady dealings, where forged identities and illicit items can be found. Access to this hidden realm is facilitated through Permanent-Entry Coins, referred to as "fast passes," which allow ongoing access to the bazaar. The Thieves Bazaar is steeped in intrigue and danger, underscored by whispered tales and the potential for both discovery and peril.

Recently, the Thieves Bazaar has revealed more of its complex character, featuring a lively arts district known as the Leaky Pit and an elite disguise and tailor shop called the Shapeshifter. This artistic atmosphere attracts a diverse clientele and serves as a hub for goods and services outside of the reach of the upper city. The access to the bazaar has also been enhanced through secret routes, including a hidden stairway revealed by Matilda Manukian, a half-elf with connections in the undercity.

Additionally, the Thieves Bazaar now encompasses an area designated for strategic planning, training, and communal military activities. This compound serves as a headquarters that features rooms for military strategy, displaying maps from historical battles and focusing on modern military tactics reminiscent of the Sekban. Within it, a bandaged leader named Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, reputedly the deceased Sekban commander, commands the Ghost of March. Discussions of unity and military strategy occur here, emphasizing collaboration with the Janissaries, led by Ahra 'the Wolf.' The compound also hosts a library focused on military history, reflecting a sophisticated understanding of warfare amidst the ongoing vibrancy of the bazaar. As the area continues to bustle with patrons engaging in various activities, it marks a sense of community among its residents, where alliances are formed and secrets are exchanged, mirroring the intricate dynamics of the undercity.
