Basilica Cistern

The Basilica Cistern, or Yerebatan Sarnıcı, is an underground structure beneath the city of Istanbul, featuring a network of cisterns and interconnecting tunnels, with some routes predating the church. It was constructed during the reign of Emperor Justinian and served as a crucial water supply for the city. The cistern has hoist holes in the floor and metal rings for rigging, indicating its historical use for water storage and possibly other purposes. It is relatively dry and well-preserved, with numerous columns that support the ceiling.

Explorers in the Basilica Cistern encountered a fresh dead Janissaries, signs of struggle, and a jammed door that was opened using magic. They discovered an old storage room filled with wooden chests, where a Giant Spider attacked one of the explorers. Further exploration revealed a steep stairwell leading to a corridor, where they spotted a moving human skull. Combat ensued with animated skeletons and a Desiccated Undead. They also met a living Janissaries named Alexander, who warned them to leave the area. The explorers took measures to prevent further undead manifestations by burning additional bodies.

More recently, it has been revealed that The Shahmeran, an entity imprisoned in the cistern since the time of Justinian and Theodora, is bound by powerful forces. The last fight against The Shahmeran and her spellcasters occurred during the reign of Justinian.
