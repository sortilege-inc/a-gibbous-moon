Undercity

The Undercity is a bustling subterranean realm accessible through Nazif's Scents in the Grand Bazaar. This labyrinthine expanse features a large stone hall adorned with carpets and a vibrant marketplace populated by tieflings, half-orcs, and half-elves. The architecture includes old stone walls and arches illuminated by various light sources, creating a lively atmosphere. The Undercity serves as a refuge for those cursed or altered by magic, offering a sense of community among its residents.

The descent from Nazif's shop leads to immediate engagement with the vibrant life within. Local guards direct newcomers to the Arraf Tavern, a focal point for exploration. Here, they can meet Kore, a singer who discusses Sufism and inclusion for non-humans, establishing connections to other residents and insights into Undercity culture. An Undercity coin pass, given by Kore, facilitates movement within this realm, reinforcing its reputation as a haven for those on the fringes of society.

The Undercity is characterized by dimly lit passages and hidden markets where a diverse range of inhabitants, including merchants and alchemists, conduct business. The climate within the Undercity is cool and damp, contrasting with the bustling atmosphere above. Notable structures include the Thieves Bazaar, where goods such as poisons, forged identities, and magical items are traded, and Sips and Florida Sea Light, a cramped rare bookshop run by a tiefling proprietor. The Undercity also provides access to essential services, such as healing potions and enchanted items, though prices can be steep, reflecting the complex nature of trade and social dynamics within this subterranean haven.

Narrow, winding alleys and densely packed structures further define the Undercity, creating a labyrinthine environment. It is populated by a range of inhabitants, including children and youth involved in petty crime, as well as adults such as thieves and muggers. These individuals operate under the oversight of an enigmatic leader known as the Ghost of March. The atmosphere is often filled with the sounds of conflict and illicit dealings, reflecting a community largely self-governed and wary of outside authority.

Notable figures include Sidra al-Najjar, Jean-Zera Mongé, Gizem Faruki, and Miray, who navigate the tight alleys in search of a perfumer's shop. During their exploration, they encounter a Frank, leading to an ambush by muggers, including an Ejder, an orc, and a tiefling. The confrontation escalates, with various attacks and magical interventions taking place, including a transformation of Ahmet into a fox. Key threats during the conflict include Mr. Karen, a dwarf leader who injures Sidra. After several exchanges, the group manages to subdue several attackers and negotiates with the remaining youth under Mr. Karen's control. They arrange for the children to be taken to safe lodging, with assistance from a contact named Kore, who has connections within the Undercity. The governance of the Undercity, under the influence of the Ghost of March, maintains a semblance of order amidst the chaos, illustrating its role as a refuge for those escaping the oppressive conditions of the upper city.

Recent discoveries reveal that the Undercity is not only a haven for the living but also a dark expanse characterized by ancient stone architecture and a labyrinthine layout. It features various chambers, some holding remnants of prior occupants, including an Undead Priest consuming the corpses of other priests. The air is thick with the scent of decay and dampness, while a hole in the ceiling suggests connections to the surface. Inhabitants include various undead entities and treasure seekers, such as Hayriah, an East African tomb-raider leader, and her crew. The Undercity serves as a repository for ancient relics and dark secrets, indicated by the presence of guardians and powerful artifacts, including a casket with broken chains and runic wards. Notable sites within the Undercity include a Laboratory and an Armory. Threats include scorpions, undead guardians, and armed men led by Hayriah, who demand the return of stolen items. The Undercity also plays a role in significant encounters, such as with a Vampire that manifests from a cold mist and is ultimately destroyed. Additionally, a ghostly figure named Radu the Handsome expresses gratitude for the defeat of his brother. The Undercity is a hub of intrigue and danger, with ongoing plans to meet with the Ghost of March.

Further exploration has revealed a complex network of hidden passages and venues, including the Hippodrome, a site for chariot races. The Hippodrome features a large central area for spectators and is imbued with enchantment that affects the entire arena while retaining its stonework. Inhabitants of the Undercity include Chique Manu, a wealthy bookmaker, and Cyrus, a gray-bearded dwarf druid who operates a bead-selling shop. At the Hippodrome, a Green-team chariot faltered due to the weakening of a spectral horse, causing frustration among the crowd. An elven woman was observed performing a necrotic debilitation on a spectral horse in the stables, suggesting potential sabotage linked to Chique Manu.

Additionally, the Styx Bathhouse has been identified as another location in the Undercity, featuring a large communal pool and private rooms, serving as a venue for networking and rumor exchange. The bathhouse operator, Hussein, offers insights into local culture and recommends it for self-care and gathering information. Recently, a compound within the Undercity has been discovered, featuring an open arched gate guarded by an orcish figure and a halfling in Sekban-adjacent fatigues. This layout includes both open-air and enclosed rooms, such as strategy spaces, a training yard, and conference areas, presenting a modern military impression. Strategy rooms display maps from historical battles, indicative of a force styled as a modernized “new army” in the Sekban mold. The compound also contains a cafeteria where uniformed recruits, likely non-human, share meals, with aromas of fresh bread and stew mingling with the scent of gunfire and explosive residue. Recruits engage in firearms training and discussions about military strategy. Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, the bandaged leader reputedly the deceased Sekban commander, discusses the need for unity among factions and presents a journal belonging to Osman, inviting the group to refer potential recruits for the new Sekban Army. The political climate emphasizes the importance of maintaining appearances regarding the Janissaries and Sekban enmity.
