Yedikule Fortress

Yedikule Fortress is a fortified structure located in Constantinople, designed as a defensive stronghold. The fortress contains several key rooms, including the Sewers (Room 12), Common Prison (Room 13), Royal Cell (Room 14), Barracks (Room 15), High Security Cells (Room 17), and Central Towers II and III (Rooms 10 and 11). The upper tower level is sealed unless unsealed for a fee. The fortress is equipped with various traps, including a sleep gas grenade, a falling net, and reinforced entrances.

The sewer entrances are reinforced with chains and contain scattered caltrops and ball bearings. This area serves as a potential infiltration route for attackers and was fortified as part of the defensive preparations for Yedikule Fortress. During the assault on Yedikule, the attackers chose not to enter through the sewers, opting instead for the main gate and ground-level entrance. The sewer entrances remained unutilized by Hamit and his crew during their approach to the fortress.

The fortress was fortified in preparation for an ambush against Hamit the Quartz of Damascus. A decoy operation was executed, placing the real Vizier, Mustafa Pasha, Vizier-i Aezam-i Azam, in a High-Security Cell (Room 17) while a decoy was escorted to the Royal Cell (Room 14). The Royal Cell serves as a guest suite designated for high-status prisoners and is used as a decoy location to mislead infiltrators and potential moles. Two guards are positioned at the door to the Royal Cell area to enhance security. The High-Security Cell is a fortified area designated for the confinement of prisoners and currently houses five common criminals. This cell was reinforced as part of broader defensive measures taken to prepare Yedikule Fortress for the assault. Additional fighters were hired and positioned throughout the fortress, including hidden veteran fighters posing as prisoners.

During the assault, Hamit and his crew approached the fortress at night, engaging in combat that resulted in several hired guards being killed. The attackers fought through the ground floor and cleared Central Tower II and Central Tower III, which includes a small barracks containing approximately ten beds. This room served as a strategic location for the defenders, with hired guards positioned there to strengthen the fortress's defenses. The Barracks also serve as the guard sleeping quarters and armory, featuring a forge for maintenance and repairs, supporting the fortress's defensive operations by providing essential resources for the guards stationed there. A Recruited Mage Prisoner cast a powerful spell before escaping, leaving the defenders at a disadvantage. The assault culminated in a chaotic battle, with the attackers sustaining injuries and considering escape routes as reinforcements arrived. More recently, the layout of Yedikule Fortress has continued to play a strategic role in its defense, with ongoing assessments of its traps and room configurations.
