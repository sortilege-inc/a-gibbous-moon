Dolmabahce New Palace Construction Site

The Dolmabahce Dolmabahce New Palace Construction Site is a pivotal location within the Empire, currently under development as a grand palace intended to host political gatherings. This site is closely associated with Abd-ul Mejid, whose invitations for meetings there highlight its significance in the complex political landscape of the time. As the construction progresses, it remains a focal point for discussions that may shape the future governance of the Empire, particularly in light of the ongoing Kavalan's Rebellion.

The site features a partially completed structure, boasting a recently finished pavilion that leads into a spacious roofed courtyard, which glows with lantern light in the early evening. The open areas are striking in their design, intended for social gatherings of high-ranking officials and figures of influence, yet still exhibit signs of construction. Notably, the newly crowned Padisha and his wife, the Sultan, were present in the courtyard during a recent significant meeting. Aziz, a spectacled official allied with Osman Hamdi Bey, introduced a group of three visitors, referred to as valuable assets cultivated by Osman. In a moment steeped in secrecy, the Sultan revealed a silver box containing a ribbon-tied scroll meant to erase any memory of the meeting from the mind of an assassin sent ahead of Kavalan Pasha's anticipated visit. Following the meeting, the trio examined the scroll, coming to the alarming realization of its powerful memory-altering capabilities.

Recent developments at the site have unveiled the complexities of its construction and the ongoing political intrigue, further emphasizing its role as a center for clandestine operations linked to the upcoming confrontation involving Kavalan Pasha and other political factions. The atmosphere surrounding the construction site thickens with tension as the hidden motives and plans of those involved grow increasingly intricate.
