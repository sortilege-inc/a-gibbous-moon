Hagia Sophia

The Hagia Sophia is a grand architectural marvel located in the heart of the city, notable for its massive dome, intricate mosaics, and expansive courtyards that serve as public gathering places. This prominent structure is not only a significant landmark but also holds considerable cultural and political importance, often hosting royal events and ceremonies. It is surrounded by various plazas and is connected to the lineage of Vlad and Radu's father, underscoring its historical significance in church history. A distinctive feature of the Hagia Sophia is a door adorned with red Easter egg shells, linked to a legend involving the last patriarch.

Recently, during the coronation of Abd-ul Mejid, the Hagia Sophia was the backdrop for the presentation of the Sword Of Osman, a pivotal ceremony that attracted large crowds. The Imperial Guard maintained order as coins were tossed into the audience. However, tensions arose when some protesters claimed that the gold belonged to the Janissaries, prompting a swift response from security personnel. The Hagia Sophia functioned as both a ceremonial site and a focal point for public sentiment and unrest, reflecting the city's complex socio-political landscape.

The area around the Hagia Sophia is frequented by individuals seeking information, including those searching for Matilda Manukian. Local customs involve delivering baked goods to shops, which can serve as a cover for inquiries. Additionally, the vicinity is associated with the Ghost of March, a figure connected to the undercity and surrounding neighborhoods, drawing interest from various parties, including Osman and the A Certain Community.
