Constantinople

Constantinople, a vibrant and historically rich city, serves as a significant cultural and political hub at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, under the rule of the Ottoman Empire in 1857. This bustling metropolis is a melting pot of diverse populations, including Sunni Muslims, Christians, and Jews, and it stands as the seat of the Orthodox Church. The city’s architecture, a testament to its Byzantine heritage, is complemented by grand libraries, artifact rooms, and residential mansions, showcasing a variety of styles that reflect its complex history. The Mediterranean climate brings warm summers and mild winters, enhancing the lively atmosphere filled with scholars, merchants, and adventurers.

To the north of Constantinople lies Thymbra Farm, a picturesque farmhouse nestled among rolling hills and archaeological dig sites. Recently, it hosted a dinner organized by the Calvert family, attended by notable figures such as Osman Hamdi Bey, a royal polymath, and young helpers from the nearby dig site, Sidra al-Najjar and Ahmet Yusuf. The evening was alive with discussions about promising archaeological finds at an ancient Greek site, with guests conversing in French, Turkish, and English, reflecting the cosmopolitan nature of the gathering. Amidst the socializing, Gizem Faruki, a Sufi dervish, captivated attendees with her whirling dance on the terrace.

However, the atmosphere took a mysterious turn when a flowering tree in front of the veranda withered overnight, shocking the guests and hinting at enigmatic forces at play in the city. The following day, Ahmet Yusuf discovered a tree with a 30-foot radius of ground leached of color, while Sidra al-Najjar sensed magical residue in the farmhouse. Concerns escalated when Heinrich Schliemann was reported missing, prompting Osman Hamdi Bey to initiate a search.

As the group ventured toward the dig site, they uncovered a narrow crevice leading to a cave with a partially collapsed stone chamber adorned with ancient frescoes predating Greek culture. Inside, they encountered a Serpent and learned of interlopers in the next chamber. Upon entering, they found Heinrich Schliemann and Frank Calvert trapped within a magical ward. After disrupting the ward and sketching the room, Heinrich insisted on opening a massive door decorated with images of Aphrodite. Rufus Dimitrious discovered a nest of valuables in the Serpent's lair, but the adventure did not end there. Upon returning to the farmhouse, Osman Hamdi Bey emphasized the legal implications of exporting artifacts from the Ottoman Empire, further underscoring the city's ongoing political and cultural significance. An urgent message summoned Osman Hamdi Bey back to Constantinople, hinting at the complex interplay of history, magic, and intrigue that defines this remarkable city.

Additionally, Topkapi Palace is located in Constantinople and serves as a royal residence and administrative center. It consists of a complex of ornate buildings and courtyards, showcasing the grandeur of the Ottoman Empire. The palace was the site of the execution of an assassin by the Egyptian Kavalan, followed by the use of Speak with Dead on the corpse. A court recess occurred after the execution, resulting in the dismissal of attendees. The corpse had its memories wiped in life but could still provide information in death.

Constantinople is also home to the Basilica Cistern and its underground infrastructure. The Basilica Cistern is flooded and features raised wooden walkways and boats. Its accessible entrance is guarded by two Mansur enforcers. Inside the Basilica Cistern, visitors may encounter a French inscription signed PG by Petrus Gyllius. The area contains a magically preserved drum and bag, a submerged mirror reflecting an East Asian soldier, and various treasures including skeletons, Lydian coins, a sealed metal box, and magical spectacles. A Medusa statue can drain the cistern and reveal stairs leading to a lower prison beneath Constantinople. This lower prison contains cursed keys, a fire elemental, and multiple imprisoned figures, including Mahidevran Hatun and a scarlet-dressed sorceress who reveals herself when freed.
