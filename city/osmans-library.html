Osman's Library

Osman's Library is a location where individuals seek knowledge, particularly regarding the Hippodrome and race-fixing. The library lacks comprehensive records on these topics. Jean-Zera Mongé found little useful material during a visit due to Osman's absence.

The library is home to Ashraf, a rude tortoise who provides insights into practical frameworks for fixing spectral races, suggesting that one-time schemes are easier to execute than sustained ones. Jean-Zera Mongé attempted to gather information about a protected diary, but the search yielded scant results. The tortoise implied that layered intermediaries could reduce exposure in race-fixing schemes. The library serves as a point of inquiry for those looking to navigate the complexities of the Undercity and its politics.

More recently, the library, known as Osman Hamdi Bey’s Library, has been recognized for its scholarly significance, housing a collection of books and journals. However, it has been selectively looted, with notable items missing, including Osman's Journal, books on ciphers, charm magic, and archaeological digs. Ashraf expresses concern over disturbances caused by visitors, and Osman Hamdi Bey has not returned home, raising questions about his whereabouts.

During a visit, the party discovers the missing items and engages with Ashraf, who mentions that Osman had recently sought help from “The Shahmeran.” Jean-Zera Mongé examines Osman’s desk and finds a secret compartment containing a folded, sealed letter bearing Osman’s seal. They also locate Osman's Journal hidden in plain sight. The sealed letter, addressed to “my dear friends,” states that Osman is likely dead and bequeaths his manor and its secrets to the party. The letter contains information about a powerful diviner and a key to her location, as well as a chess metaphor from Osman.
