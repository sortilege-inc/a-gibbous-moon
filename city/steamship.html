Steamship

The Steamship is a busy vessel of no government's flag, famed for the liveliness of its company and its spirit of good fellowship. At its heart lies the Parlor Deck, a spirited quarter where passengers gather about oblong tables at cards and dinner whilst a band fills the air with music, to the improvement of every acquaintance. The deck is fitted with sleeping-berths held in common, which breeds a camaraderie among travellers even as it wants the privacy of finer accommodation.

The Steamship is, however, no mere conveyance, but a stage for the unlooked-for. So it proved when Osman Hamdi Bey was accosted by a woman in seeming distress, and was led with others to look into a nearby cabin. What appeared an innocent errand turned of a sudden to peril when the woman showed herself a wicked Shade, and there broke out a struggle of spell and steel. The scene grew the tenser when a working cast by a Singer plunged the deck into darkness and confounded their handling of the threat; and the while, the ship began to take on water from a spell rebounded, so that swift action was wanted to stave off disaster.

Thus the Steamship, in its mingling of ease and hazard, stands as a signal setting for both the pleasures of society and the terrors of high adventure — a fair emblem of the caprice of life upon the water.
