Nazif’s Perfumery

Nazif's Perfumery is a dim-lit shop in the Grand Bazaar, known for the muted musk of its several fragrances that hangs upon the air. In earlier days it served as a way into the undercity, kept by Nazif, a perfumer lately killed during the Festival of Candles; its shelves are ranked with vials and jars of scent, and its whole atmosphere is a subdued one.

The perfumery now answers to a black cat named Sharif the Strong Fur, who oversees its business in exchange for coin or a password, and who has laid down a rule against all magic against the volatile times that follow the festival. He has claimed the place for his own authority, and has asked besides for aid on behalf of four cats gravely ill. Sharif let it be known that Nazif had been relieved of his duties by the Ghost of March, and maintained that every cat reports to him, citing a folk-tale of the town in which the cats overcame the monkeys of Istanbul. All of which serves to mark the perfumery for a critical door into the undercity amidst the shifting powers that have followed Nazif's murder.
