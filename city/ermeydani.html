Ermeydani

Ermeydani is an underground fighting-ground where no weapons are borne, famed for its heavy betting and its clamorous sporting air. It runs after the manner of an arena of unarmed combat, drawing a various crowd willing to contend without armour or blade. The place is marked by the ardour of its patrons, drawn to the thrill of the bouts and the fellowship amongst the onlookers.

Sidra al-Najjar of late mapped the undercity and marked out Ermeydani as a notable station within the pleasure-quarter that gathers about the Leaky Pit — that explosive arts district, frequented by visitors of the upper class come to enjoy its several performances and diversions. Ermeydani stands apart as a place where any man may take part or look on, and adds its share to the lively and open humour of the undercity.
