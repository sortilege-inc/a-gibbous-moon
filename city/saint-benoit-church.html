Saint Benoit Church

Saint Benoit Church is an abandoned Catholic church dating back to the 1400s, recognized as the first of its kind in the region. It fell into disrepair after an earthquake approximately 120 years ago, resulting in its abandonment. The structure remains in ruins, and locals report disturbances, including alleged demon infestations, unsettling noises at night, and sightings of the undead. These tales are often linked to a parashad from a century ago and rumored treasures associated with the site.

Local inquiries reveal the church's historical significance and its eerie reputation, with townsfolk warning against entering due to supposed supernatural occurrences. A detailed map indicates several entrances, including a graveyard access point leading into the church.

Exploration of the church led to the discovery of an unmarked trapdoor in the cemetery, which accessed a series of subterranean corridors not shown on their map, indicating a later-added basement beneath the original church level. As they explored deeper, they encountered a suit of armor guarding chests, animated cadavers, and various chambers, including a torture room and a storeroom with silver pieces. The exploration revealed the presence of undead and a spectral audience, culminating in a ghostly encounter where a spirit requested assistance in confronting his brother, Vlad, who was bound within the church. Ghost of March warned about the dangers of shedding blood, which would empower Vlad, and requested their help in breaking the seals that confined him.
