Rufus Dimitrious

Rufus Dimitrious, a Greek sailor turned rogue of the Secret Service subclass, is a man of many layers, marked by his distinctive half-cape made of sailcloth and a past intertwined with the independence movement. Currently, he navigates the murky waters of smuggling logistics, a role he embraces with discretion, especially during social gatherings. At a dinner hosted at Thymbra Farm, Rufus Dimitrious is introduced by Frank Calvert as a trusted coastal guide, a clever facade that conceals his deeper involvement in illicit activities.

As the evening unfolds, Rufus Dimitrious listens intently to the conversations of notable guests like General Patrice de MacMahon and Osman Hamdi Bay, who exchange commendations on various efforts. After the meal, he discreetly receives a payment of 50 gold from Frank Calvert for arranging smuggling routes and scouting coastal coves. Tasked with fetching Heinrich Schliemann at Calvert's request, Rufus Dimitrious attempts to eavesdrop on a private conversation between Calvert and Schliemann, but a creaking floorboard alerts them, prompting a swift closure of the door.

Later, Rufus Dimitrious entertains guests such as Sidra al-Najjar and Ahmet Yusuf with tall tales of hunting mermaids and riding leviathans, though his stories are met with skepticism. The following morning, he shares breakfast drinks with General de MacMahon, both nursing hangovers from the previous night’s festivities. Their camaraderie is abruptly interrupted when a nearby flowering tree withers instantly, casting an unsettling shadow over their surroundings.

As an ex-sailor and smuggler, Rufus Dimitrious is part of a group that includes Ahmet Yusuf, Jean-Zera Mongé, Sidra al-Najjar, and Osman Hamdi Bay, often discussing potential loot and smuggling opportunities. During an investigation at a farmhouse near Constantinople, he encouraged a French general to touch a colorless tree, though it yielded no immediate effects. Rufus Dimitrious later reported overheard information about "a hill and a key," hinting at its importance to their expedition.

In a daring plan to break into a locked study, Rufus Dimitrious successfully picked the lock, uncovering shelves filled with literature, a marble bust of Homer, and a spare key hidden in the desk. His explorations led him to a cave where he discovered a narrow crevice that opened to a hidden passage. Descending into a Serpent's hole, he found a nest of valuables, including a silver owl brooch, which he cleverly manipulated to temporarily transform into a real owl.

Rufus Dimitrious also interacted with sailors on a Non-Government Steamer, reconnecting with a Greek Captain from his past who warned him about a black-market fixer named Janus the Cut, advising him to keep his dealings away from Osman Hamdi Bay. Later, he played a crucial role in confronting a wicked Shade aboard a ship, firing at it from a doorway while his companions engaged in combat. Afterward, he expressed excitement about their recent loot, discussing the treasures from the Serpent's nest with Jean-Zera Mongé and arranging the sale of certain items to Osman Hamdi Bay.

As a trusted intermediary with connections in the underworld of Constantinople, Rufus Dimitrious operates within the city's social and political networks, facilitating communication and transactions between parties involved in clandestine activities. He is familiar with the criminal elements of the city and has a past working relationship with influential figures such as Osman Hamdi Bey and the writer Chique Manu. Tasked with delivering a coded letter using illusory script to Jean-Zera Mongé's father, he ensures that sensitive information regarding the political situation is communicated discreetly. Rufus Dimitrious also made an appointment with Chique Manu, further solidifying his role in connecting the party with significant contacts in the city.
