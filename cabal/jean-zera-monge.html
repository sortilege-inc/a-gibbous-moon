Player Character

Jean-Zera Mongé

✦ Open the Character Sheet

Jean-Zera Mongé, a half-elf bard and antiquarian, is a captivating figure whose charm and artistic flair are evident in his modern trousers and dinner jacket, notably devoid of a tie. His long, curly white hair adds to his ambiguous appearance, making it challenging for others to determine whether he is in his late twenties or late fifties. With a background steeped in antiquities, having worked alongside his father in Napoleonic Egypt, Jean-Zera has honed his magical skills to aid his pursuits in this field.

Currently, he finds himself at a dig site in the Dardanelles, preparing to meet with a British contact named Frank Calvert. This meeting is part of his ongoing endeavors that connect him to his father's business associates in Constantinople. Recently, at a dinner hosted by the Calvert family at Thymbra Farm, Jean-Zera mingled effortlessly with guests, showcasing his talents by sketching attendees and playing piano pieces that enhanced the evening's atmosphere. During this gathering, he engaged in a conversation with General Patrice de MacMahon, expressing a keen interest in an archaeological dig that had uncovered a megaron, a significant civic building from an ancient Greek city. His friendly disposition was on full display as he interacted with children at the event, gifting them sketches he had made. Throughout the evening, Jean-Zera's role extended beyond mere socialization; he sought to gather valuable information about the archaeological site, engaging with various guests, including the notable Osman Hamdi Bay, all while maintaining an air of intrigue and sophistication befitting his eclectic background.

In addition to his social endeavors, Jean-Zera is a scholarly occultist recognized for his expertise in arcane studies and ancient history. Methodical in his investigations, he often examines texts and artifacts with a keen intellect and a strong curiosity about historical relics. He has identified lingering transmutation magic associated with a desaturated tree and has engaged with ancient texts, notably a hidden scroll in ancient Greek titled “The Lion’s King,” which he discovered contained flaws and miscopied sections. His explorations have led him to investigate a farmhouse and its surroundings, where he heard a loud crack and identified magical residues. Upon returning to the farmhouse, he retrieved cartographer's tools and gained access to a locked study, uncovering extensive research related to Troy. In a hidden compartment, he found a spell scroll and took a spare key from the desk. He also assessed cave frescoes, recognizing their archaeological significance as predating ancient Greek culture.

During an encounter with a Serpent, Jean-Zera observed the creature's interaction with his companions and participated in discussions about the potential dangers and mysteries of the site. He sketched a magical ward found within a chamber and interpreted its relation to the earlier discovered spell. After an incident involving Heinrich Schliemann, who attempted to open a massive door, Jean-Zera advised caution and suggested returning with appropriate resources. He made a clean copy of the spell scroll before returning the original to the study, retaining the key for himself. His expertise has proven invaluable in evaluating relics found by the group, assisting Rufus Dimitrious in identifying their significance and facilitating their sale to Osman Hamdi Bay.

Jean-Zera is also involved in the political and social dynamics of his setting, particularly focusing on religious discussions regarding women's roles in religious practices. He is part of a community that encourages dialogue on these issues and has connections to influential figures, including Osman Hamdi Bay. Seeking to recover a stolen journal of significant importance, he possesses magical abilities, demonstrated by his use of spells such as detect magic and illusory script for discreet communication. He plays an active role during the transition of power following the death of The Caliph, expressing interest in the stolen journal and exploring its magical implications. As part of his mission to recover the journal, Jean-Zera delivers a sealed letter to Ahra “the Wolf,” a Janissaries leader, and infiltrates criminal operations. He also composes a coded report to his father using illusory script, demonstrating his intent to convey critical information discreetly. His investigative nature is evident as he observes and follows a suspicious individual offering lodging to a homeless man, reflecting a commitment to understanding the complexities of the situation and concern for the safety and well-being of his community.

In moments of tension, Jean-Zera demonstrates an ability to blend into his surroundings, showcasing stealth and cunning. In a confrontation with an albasti, he casts Dissonant Whispers, inflicting psychic pain on a human helper and causing him to flee. He also uses Healing Word to restore his ally, Ahmet, bringing him back to consciousness with low strength. Observant and cautious, Jean-Zera assesses threats before taking action, whispering encouragement to Ahmet, showcasing his support and tactical awareness. Following the confrontation with the albasti, he engages in research at Digadas’ library, seeking information on cursed sleep and magical afflictions.

During a visit to a clandestine gambling club, Jean-Zera was seen with Gizem Faruki, who adopted the male persona "Gizem Faruki," leading others to perceive them as partners. His attire and demeanor reflect a comfort with the diverse clientele of the club, which includes older, cosmopolitan men and a notable French woman. The club is characterized by lush decor, card tables, and a backgammon table, creating an atmosphere of sophistication and competition. In the gambling club, Jean-Zera engaged with the surrounding dynamics, observing the play and remaining attuned to the interactions unfolding around them.

After a tense card game with high stakes, Jean-Zera became involved in a physical confrontation during an abduction attempt involving Chique Manu. They closed the distance to engage in melee combat, delivering multiple unarmed strikes to the assailant, Rashad. Despite sustaining injuries from a risky maneuver, Jean-Zera continued to fight, ultimately knocking Rashad unconscious and aiding in the rescue of Chique Manu. His actions in the Undercity reveal a proactive mindset, as he purchased healing potions for the party members, demonstrating a sense of responsibility for the group's well-being.

Jean-Zera navigates the chaotic environment of the Undercity, actively searching for a buyer for a peculiar book about himself. His observant nature enables him to notice a Frank attempting to pickpocket him in a crowded alley. He interacts with various entities, including an Ejder, an Elvish Woman, and a dwarf leader known as Mr. Karen. During an ambush by muggers, Jean-Zera uses Unsettling Words and casts Dissonant Whispers on the Ejder, inflicting pain and causing the creature to stagger. However, he is ultimately knocked unconscious by an Orc Brute after attempting to reposition himself. After a Tiefling Caster stabilizes him, Jean-Zera recovers and participates in the aftermath of the encounter, redistributing a heavy coin purse found on Mr. Karen to the children under the dwarf's care. He confronts the chaotic Frank, successfully disarming him and reclaiming the money for the children.

Recently, Jean-Zera has showcased his skills as a knife-wielding martial artist, known for his precise combat style. He has emerged from battles covered in the remnants of undead foes, creating confusion and fear among his adversaries. In an encounter with an Undead Priest, he executed two precise cuts that disemboweled and killed the creature. He then exited the room past Sidra al-Najjar, flicking the gore from his knife against the wall to intimidate the armed men present, startling one who mistakenly believed Jean-Zera to be a zombie and shot him, resulting in a wound. Despite this, Jean-Zera refused to kneel or disarm, countering by driving his elbows into two of the escorts to block their advance.

In subsequent battles, Jean-Zera demonstrated his martial prowess by engaging swarms of scorpions, dispatching them with dagger strikes and martial blows. He deflected a venomous sting from a grappling scorpion and cut down the broodmother scorpion, making significant contributions to the party's efforts by landing strikes against various foes, including a Mounted Warrior and a Janissaries, aiding in the defeat of several guardians encountered during their exploration. Moreover, Jean-Zera possesses a keen intellect and resourcefulness, utilizing his surroundings to gather information and navigate complex social situations. He is affiliated with Osman, a significant figure in local power dynamics, and has been involved in dealings that attract the attention of various factions, including a Community's A Certain Community. Jean-Zera carries one of two uncursed Magical Short Swords (+1) taken from a hoard, indicating his readiness for conflict.

Recently, he was approached by well-dressed men who inquired about his connection to Osman and Giannis, pressing a concealed firearm into his ribs and insisting he accompany them. He detected a mental probe from one of the agents, who sought to gauge his legal standing as a foreigner. Despite the threat, he remained composed, and with the subtle assistance of Sidra, who cast Guidance and opened a Message link, he managed to navigate the situation. Sidra's charm spell altered the lead agent's perception, allowing Jean-Zera to escape.

Later, Jean-Zera found himself in a carriage with an older man, the head of a A Certain Community apparatus, who urged him to influence Osman to cease certain activities deemed disruptive to the Empire's harmony. The official demanded secrecy regarding their conversation and hinted at the involvement of Kavalan Pasha, suggesting deeper entanglements for Jean-Zera's family. After disembarking near Giannis's gate, Jean-Zera evaded guards and a tail, demonstrating his ability to maneuver through perilous circumstances. He later received a disguise at the Shapeshifter, transforming his appearance to that of a Turkish man, which would aid him in future encounters.

In his recent endeavors, Jean-Zera has exhibited a tactical demeanor and a cautious approach to situations. He is observant, particularly regarding military uniforms, which he notes may be newly made to appear old or actual surplus. He possesses a keen analytical mind, demonstrated when he examines a journal and identifies a complex cipher with a magical ward or glamor component, indicating significant skill or magic is required to decode it. Jean-Zera interacts with various figures, including Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, discussing the potential for a united front against unspecified adversaries. He is aware of being followed but maintains a circumspect attitude. He demonstrates a willingness to assist others, casting Lesser Restoration on Shehrazat, a young tiefling suffering from blood rot, and discussing finding a tutor to help her control her innate magic.

Recently, Jean-Zera attended the spectral Hippodrome chariot races with Yasmine, a contact with limited knowledge of the Undercity. He conversed with Chique Manu, a wealthy bookmaker, discussing wagers and socializing. At the races, Jean-Zera observed the performance of the Green Team and noted the faltering of a spectral horse, which contributed to the team's poor finish. He created a staged distraction to pull Chique Manu away, allowing for an opportunity to disengage from him. After the race, Jean-Zera sought an introduction to an elven woman seen whispering to Chique Manu, which Chique Manu agreed to facilitate.

Later, Jean-Zera visited Osman's Library to search for records related to the Hippodrome and race-fixing but found little useful material. He also planned to discreetly move a fake journal to Aziz, ensuring that allies would not be implicated. Throughout these events, Jean-Zera demonstrated a keen interest in Undercity politics and the dynamics of the spectral races.

As a member of a party on a mission to identify an assassin known as the Kavalan Of Egypt, Jean-Zera possesses magical abilities, including chill touch and vicious mockery, which he uses in combat. He is recognized for his strategic thinking and actively participates in discussions regarding their objectives and potential threats. During the mission, he inspected a reinforced door and attempted entry, triggering an arrow trap that struck his companion. He engaged in combat against a Desiccated Undead, using chill touch to impose disadvantage on its next attack. He also used unnerving words to force a Janissaries defector named Alexander back during a tense standoff, ultimately helping to subdue him without lethal force. After the confrontation, Jean-Zera escorted Alexander to a safe location in the Undercity, where they received temporary lodging. He conducted research on the Janissaries and the Sekban, noting the modernization shift under the Potasha’s Line. Jean-Zera arranged a meeting with a representative of the assassin at The Pearl, proposing a decoy plot to misdirect attention from the real target. He used a Memory-Wipe Scroll to erase the representative's recollection of their conversation, ensuring the mission could proceed without complications.

More recently, Jean-Zera possesses a square piece of sturdy paper featuring an ornate sigil or monogram, resembling that found on a signet ring. He feels a sense of terror regarding this paper, fearing it has been misplaced or taken from him. In a dream, he recalls the sigil as a marker of identity that would be emblazoned on his possessions. The following morning, he sketches the sigil from memory, creating a passable approximation. While investigating Osman Hamdi Bey’s library, Jean-Zera examines a desk and discovers a trapped latch, successfully opening a secret compartment that contains a folded, sealed letter bearing Osman’s seal. He reads the letter addressed to "my dear friends," which states that Osman is likely dead and bequeaths his manor and its secrets to the party. During a confrontation, Jean-Zera uses magic to attack shadowy, semi-corporeal soldiers, employing chill-inducing spectral force and enchanted bolts to destroy some of them.

In addition to his various magical abilities, Jean-Zera is recognized as a party spellcaster. He has used Mage Hand multiple times, recognized a French inscription as Petrus Gyllius’s mark, attuned to and played a recovered hand drum during a short rest, and identified a recovered scroll as a scroll of Greater Restoration. He convinced the cistern guards to let the group pass, detected abjuration on a hanging burlap bag, tugged it with Mage Hand, and hauled it up. He retrieved two Lydian gold coins with Mage Hand, read Petrus Gyllius’s diary, and attempted a psychic assault on a Merrow that resisted. He was struck in the back of the head by a thrown stone, fell into spiked water while crossing, and climbed out onto the far side. He summoned a swarm of secretary birds around the Medusa, was wounded by the Medusa’s ranged attack, and used Mage Hand to operate a cursed keyring to unlock a cell safely. He triggered a second cursed lock that exploded, badly burning him and Miray, shut and resecured that door, and fought to smother lingering fire on himself.
