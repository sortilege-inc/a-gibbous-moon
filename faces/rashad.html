The Folk of the City

Rashad

Rashad, a kabadayi, is known for his rough demeanor and adherence to an informal code. He participates in high-stakes gambling at a clandestine club, where he plays cards with notable players including Gizem Faruki and Chique Manu. Rashad displays an irritable nature, particularly as he remains winless while others succeed. He has a strong build typical of street fighters and wields a blade, which he uses to threaten Chique Manu during an attempted abduction.

During the card game, Rashad's frustration escalates as he continues to lose, leading him to resort to violence by pressing a knife to Chique Manu's throat and attempting to drag him toward a concealed exit. This triggers a series of confrontations, culminating in a melee engagement where he is attacked by Jean-Zera Mongé and distracted by Gizem Faruki's spell. Despite his initial aggression and attempts to escape, he is ultimately subdued through coordinated strikes and knocked unconscious. Afterward, Chique Manu places a single para coin in Rashad's hand as a mocking reward for his failed attempt at abduction.
