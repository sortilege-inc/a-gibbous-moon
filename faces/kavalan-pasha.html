Kavalan Pasha

Egyptian Kavalan

Kavalan Pasha is the Egyptian governor entangled with various foreign powers, attempting to leverage their interests against Abd-ul Mejid. However, his political maneuvers have been outmatched, leading to a precarious position within the tumultuous landscape of the Empire. A high-ranking official has pointed to potential troubles involving Claude Mongé, signifying Kavalan's deep ties to the political intrigues surrounding Osman. His actions, including territorial seizures and failed suppression efforts, have captured the attention of Jean-Zera Mongé, who is investigating Kavalan's rebellion and the isolation imposed on him by the prior Padishah through alliances with Russia, France, and England.

Kavalan, a skilled politician associated with the Sublime Porte, faces scrutiny from various factions. His political actions are deemed problematic, complicating the existing power dynamics within the region. Recently, he escalated his involvement in the undercurrents of power by sending an assassin to the city ahead of his visit. During a meeting with the Sultan and a trio of associates, the Sultan indicated that there would be no foreseeable interference from Kavalan regarding the operations of this assassin. Furthermore, the Sultan presented a silver box containing a scroll linked to Kavalan's plans, designed to erase the trio's memories of their meeting with the assassin, thereby safeguarding the integrity of the operation.

In a recent turn, Kavalan Pasha revealed himself as an assassin, associated with defectors from the Janissaries. He has dispatched agents ahead, showcasing both his influence and operational abilities. His intrigues now include a plot against Mustafa Pasha, Vizier-i Aezam, where he intends to create a diversion while concealing the real target. The Sublime Porte, seeking to uncover Kavalan's identity during a mission, utilized a Memory-Wipe Scroll to erase a representative's recollection of their conversation, allowing his plot to advance without interruption. Kavalan's actions are intricately connected to a larger scheme that manipulates the political power dynamics within the city, highlighting his pivotal role amid the complex webs of alliances and rivalries that define the city's underbelly.

More recently, Kavalan became involved in the political intrigue of the Ottoman Empire, specifically linked to the assassination plot against Hamit the Quartz of Damascus. During a diplomatic meeting, Kavalan rose, drew a hidden blade, and swiftly slit Hamit's throat. He cast a spell on the dying Hamit, causing his eyes to glow pale blue, similar to Speak with Dead, allowing him to question Hamit in his final moments. Kavalan suspected that Hamit's memories had been wiped and killed him to circumvent this effect and probe for palace manipulation. His actions implicated the Topkapi Palace in potential foul play, which he leveraged to strengthen his position in the ongoing diplomatic talks. His decision to murder Hamit served as both a means of disposal and a tool for intelligence gathering, leaving the peace talks in a strained state.

Kavalan also executed a would-be assassin at Topkapi Palace and possesses the ability to cast Speak with Dead, which he used on the corpse of the assassin. After the execution, the court was dismissed. The corpse revealed that its memories had been wiped in life but could still provide information in death, including a description of Jean-Zera Mongé. The party later checked on Osman Hamdi Bey’s House and library. At Osman's Library, Kavalan examined Osman’s desk, located a secret compartment, and opened it to find a folded, sealed letter bearing Osman’s seal. During an encounter with shadowy, semi-corporeal Janissaries soldiers, the Kavalan Pasha used magic to assault the shades at range, employing chill-inducing spectral force and enchanted bolts to destroy some of them.
