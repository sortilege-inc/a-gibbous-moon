Greek Captain

the Greek captain

The Greek Captain is a veteran smuggler with a background in the Greek War of Independence. He has a salty demeanor and shares a rapport with Rufus Dimitrious, indicating a history of camaraderie and mutual respect. He is involved in black-market activities, with a contact named Janus the Cut, known for expertise in illicit trade in Constantinople. The Captain cautions Rufus to keep his dealings with Janus separate from his association with Osman Hamdi Bey, emphasizing the Ottoman authority's strict stance against looting and artifact exportation.

The Captain converses with Rufus about the situation on the ship, hinting at expectations of goods from an excavation that did not materialize. He expresses disdain for those who remove artifacts from the empire, reflecting a commitment to preserving cultural history. His interactions suggest a practical and cautious nature, particularly in navigating the complexities of smuggling operations while maintaining connections.
