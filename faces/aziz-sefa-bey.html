The Folk of the City

Aziz Sefa Bey

The head of a Community's A Certain Community is a highly educated, multilingual state security chief named Aziz Sefa Bey. He is an older man with glasses and a gray beard, fitting the profile of a significant figure within the government. Known for his formal manner of speaking, Aziz is closely connected to the political landscape, particularly regarding the Empire's governance. He serves as a mediator in political matters and is allied with Osman Hamdi Bey. Aziz seeks a unified Turkey and maintains a non-prejudicial stance towards various minority religions, focusing on order.

Aziz urged Jean-Zera to influence Osman to cease what he termed "ecumenical flights," emphasizing the need for a strong, singular voice within the Empire to maintain harmony. He expressed concerns that Osman listens to too many voices, which he believes creates confusion. The official insisted on secrecy regarding their conversation, stating that the service would gather any necessary information independently. He also mentioned "Kavalan Pasha," the embattled Egyptian governor, implying that Claude Mongé might be involved in related troubles.

In a significant meeting with the newly crowned Padisha and the Sultan, Aziz introduced the trio as assets cultivated by Osman. He explained that Kavala Pasha had sent a Skilled Assassin ahead of his visit and outlined the task for the trio to identify the assassin’s tactical position without altering the plan. Aziz provided a silver box containing a scroll intended to erase any memory of the meeting from the assassin’s mind after contact. He also offered two addresses for secure follow-up: one a government office and the other a concealed Janissary safehouse in a poor district.

During the meeting, Aziz emphasized the importance of the operation and the need to catch the assassin in the act. He articulated the political dynamics at play, noting that the Sublime Porte cannot foresee any interference from Kavala Pasha. After the meeting, he thanked the trio for their service and expressed confidence in their abilities.

In a dream experienced by Gizem Faruki, Aziz appears as a younger version of himself, escorting her to the Sublime Porte in Istanbul. His name arises in discussions about the political landscape, particularly concerning the Padishah and the influence of various factions within the empire. He is mentioned in conversations about alliances and the potential dangers posed by powerful entities, such as The Shahmeran, with which he is implied to have connections.

Aziz Sefa Bey later turned openly against the party. As they crossed to the mainland and made for Mira's farmhouse near the archaeological dig, he lay in wait with riflemen of the Bostanji Imperial Guard and a secret-service escort, himself racing to reach and kill Mira—the diviner whose death would free Haseki Sultan Bergüzar Hatun's full power—before the party could shield her. Sidra al-Najjar, reading his mind through the Crown of Sargon, named him the ambush's calm, unhurried leader and learned his purpose. Walking into the firefight with cane and monocle, he put shots at Jean-Zera Mongé's feet without trying to kill—"I would have thought you would have learned from our last meeting"—but, shaken and outmatched as the party downed his men, he worked a ring into a cloud of fog and vanished, leaving the road to Mira open.
