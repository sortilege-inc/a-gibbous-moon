Frederick Calvert

Frederick Calvert

Frederick Calvert is a distinguished host at Thymbra Farm, situated to the north of Constantinople. He plays an integral role in the local archaeological excavation of an ancient Greek site, often collaborating with notable figures like Heinrich Schliemann and his brother, Frank Calvert. Married to Evelyn Calvert, Frederick is characterized by his polite demeanor and an affinity for discussing politics and trade, particularly during elegant yet informal dinners at his farmhouse.

At these gatherings, he engages with various guests, including Osman Hamdi Bay, General Patrice de MacMahon, and a German couple, Heinrich and Sophia. Conversations often revolve around the archaeological dig, especially noteworthy after a recent festival reduced the crew's numbers. Frederick's interest in the findings is evident as he navigates discussions, contributing insights while primarily communicating in French, with occasional exchanges in Turkish and English.

Frederick is also heavily invested in the investigation of a significant crack near his farmhouse, showcasing his authority in the field. He demonstrates a level of trust and leadership by granting permission for Osman Hamdi Bay to lead a group of younger participants to address urgent matters at the excavation site. His oversight is crucial, as he focuses on facilitating the group's actions and shaping their decisions regarding the exploration of ancient Troy and the exportation of artifacts from the Ottoman Empire. Frederick's presence is significant, forming a respectful bridge between the intricate dynamics of archaeological exploration and the broader implications of their discoveries.
