The Folk of the City

Sidra's Djinn

Sidra al-Najjar possesses a jinn that provides her with non-verbal insights and warnings. She demonstrates a protective nature, particularly towards those in need, as shown when she offers her cloak to Shehrazat, a young tiefling suffering from blood rot. Sidra's Djinn communicates a sense of urgency regarding Shehrazat's condition, indicating that while the illness may pass, it poses a significant risk to her life.

In the Undercity, Sidra is involved in a mission to assist Shehrazat, who has been ill for nearly two weeks following an involuntary surge of innate magic. Sidra seeks remedies from Papa Mert, an alchemist, and participates in a ritual to apply a healing paste to Shehrazat's affected areas. Throughout these events, Sidra remains focused on providing aid and support.

More recently, Sidra has been noted as a dark-visioned member of her party. She cast a spell to soften the group's movements and inspected a burlap bag on a platform, where she identified two topical anti-petrification potions among recovered bottles. Additionally, she offered baklava to local spirits and inquired with the guards about what lived below. During an encounter, she turned and slapped at a Bichura that tried to pick her pocket, apologized, and placed baklava on the boards, which then lifted and disappeared.

In a subsequent incident, Sidra fell into spiked water when a boardwalk collapsed but was pulled back up onto the far side by Gizem. She attempted to cast Charm Person on a nearby Merrow, which was not a valid target, and then successfully cast Sleep to incapacitate the nearest Merrow. Later, she burned a Medusa with sacred flame and healed herself.
