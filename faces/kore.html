The Folk of the City

Kore

Kore, a human-presenting woman, captivates audiences at the Arraf Tavern in the Undercity with her stunning singing voice. Known for her welcoming demeanor, she often arranges flowers on stage before her performances, creating a warm atmosphere that draws in patrons from various backgrounds, including tieflings and half-orcs. Kore is not only a performer but also a community advocate, particularly for vulnerable youths. Her practical approach was evident when she intervened during a tense situation involving a group of children influenced by a dwarf gang leader named Mr. Karen, arranging safe lodging for them and requiring a payment of 20 gold for their protection.

Kore's connection to the local community is strong; she provides Undercity coin passes to those she wishes to assist and engages in discussions about spiritual practices, particularly Sufism. During a visit from Gizem, she expressed her interest in religious instruction and offered guidance on navigating the Undercity, cautioning against name-dropping her to avoid extortion. Her influence extends to a figure known as the Ghost of March, who maintains order within the Undercity. Kore described Alemdar Mustafa Pasha as a legitimate leader whose presence is respected by the local populace, and she has agreed to advocate for the group of children to meet him, further showcasing her role as a bridge between different communities.

Kore is often accompanied by her large half-orc associate, Mervhan, who assists her in serving food and drink to her guests. Her presence in the Undercity signifies a blend of cultural and spiritual influences, contributing to the vibrant atmosphere of the tavern. Recently, Kore has become more involved with various factions within the Undercity, including the Ghost of March. She favors guarantees of safety and freedom of movement while preserving the integrity of Undercity spaces. During a meeting in the hall of the Ghost's compound, she warmly greeted a group and facilitated their introduction to Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, clarifying that her occasional referrals to him do not imply any patronage ties. Kore also expressed her excitement about the upcoming "specters" horse race, revealing her partiality to a horse named Blue, although her performance commitments would prevent her from attending.

In a different context, Kore is revealed to be a non-human entity who performs as part of a band, weaving a Darkness spell into her lyrics. Her appearance suggests a sense of otherness, recognized by Sidra al-Najjar, who notes that she is not wholly human. During an encounter in a cabin, she initially appears as a woman in distress but then transforms into a wicked Shade, indicating her connection to darker magic. When Osman Hamdi Bey and others enter the cabin, Kore warns Rufus Dimitrious to "seek out the Ghost of March" before disappearing below decks. This interaction highlights her enigmatic nature and deeper involvement in unfolding events, as she and her accomplices perform a teleportation ritual, showcasing her proficiency in magic and contributing to the mystery surrounding her true intentions and nature.

More recently, Kore, also known as Cori, has shown her loyalty to the Blue Team in the chariot races, which has influenced her interactions, including a conversation with Jean-Zera Mongé who sought her advice on racing tips. While she reiterated her support for the Blue Team, she did not provide actionable insights. Cori is a participant at the Spectral Hippodrome who engages with others in the stands, expressing a belief that the Blue Team is the underdog and participates in the tradition of shouting "Joseph!" at the rider to encourage them, noting that this tradition may have a more jeering quality than cheering. In her conversations, she mentions attending the Hippodrome with a friend named Yasmine and discusses their preference for the Blue Team, indicating that they place bets on the team when possible. Cori acknowledges the importance of food, suggesting they chose to eat rather than gamble on this occasion. Additionally, her feelings for another character have begun to shape her relationships within the Undercity, adding another layer to her already complex persona.
