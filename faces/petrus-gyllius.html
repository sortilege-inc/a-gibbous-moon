Petrus Gyllius

PG

Petrus Gyllius was a 16th-century French scholar, topographer, translator, and explorer known for his travels to the Basilica cistern region. He used the initials "PG" as a mark. A French inscription carved into the cistern wall, signed "PG," was identified by Jean-Zera as Gillius's mark. The party recovered what was treated as Petrus Gyllius’s diary, which records a water-transference mechanism, references stairs below the cistern, states that Justinian likely disposed of magic users’ bodies beneath the cistern, mentions a huge cage, refers to traveling companions as members of a lodge, and concludes with a reference to a dreaded she-devil.
