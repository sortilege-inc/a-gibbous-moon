The Folk of the City

Ejder

In the shadowy depths of the Undercity, the figure known as Ejder looms large, a menacing presence with draconic features that strike fear into the hearts of those who cross their path. Ejder, part of a notorious group of muggers, employs a sling-like device to launch flat stones at unsuspecting victims, showcasing a tactical approach to combat that often involves striking from partial cover.

During a chaotic encounter in a crowded alley, Ejder and their companions, which include an Elvish Woman and an Orc Brute, surround a group of adventurers. With an imposing stature, Ejder brandishes a weapon of poor quality, moving strategically to flank and intimidate the party. When threatened, they advance, demanding money with a fierce determination.

The situation escalates when Ejder is struck by Jean-Zera Mongé's spell, Dissonant Whispers, causing them to stagger back in pain. In the ensuing chaos, Ejder seizes the opportunity to grab the cowering Elvish Woman, retreating while shouting in a language reminiscent of thieves' cant. This retreat, coupled with their earlier involvement in the ambush, highlights Ejder's tactical awareness and the aggressive intentions of their group, solidifying their role as a formidable adversary in the Undercity.
