Giannis the Cut

Giannis the Cut

Giannis the Cut, a black-market fixer in Constantinople, is a notorious figure deeply entrenched in the world of smuggling and criminal activities. His involvement in the illicit trade of artifacts and antiquities paints him as a man of dubious morality, navigating a realm that intertwines with the lower echelons of society. While recognized for his skill in facilitating underground transactions, those who know him advise caution; a ship captain once warned Rufus Dimitrious to steer clear of dealings that might oppose Osman Hamdi Bey's principles against looting.

Giannis's reputation is complex, characterized by a precarious relationship with the law and a keen awareness of the cultural significance that his operations often undermine. The party, tasked with recovering a stolen journal, realizes they must infiltrate his operations, as he stands as one of the key figures in the underworld, alongside others such as Hano 'the Widow'. While not merely a killer for hire, Giannis operates within a labyrinthine web of crime, embodying both opportunities and dangers in his dealings.

He is associated with the Andropoulos Estate and is involved in dealings related to curios and artifacts. Giannis presents himself with an air of wealth and influence, showcasing legal curiosities within his jurisdiction and offering marble statues for sale at 2,500 gold each. Adopting a diplomatic approach to negotiations, he is well aware of the competitive landscape in treasure hunting, noting competition from an entity named Hayriah.

In his interactions, Giannis engages in banter and humor, particularly regarding incidents from the Festival of Candles, such as Chique Manu's near theft of his ring by shadows. He maintains cordial relationships with others, offering drinks and participating in light-hearted conversation. Giannis acknowledges the urgency surrounding Ghost of March of March and expresses interest in treasure-seeking endeavors, indicating a willingness to collaborate while negotiating terms that favor his interests.

Recently, he proposed a cut of 25% for his involvement if Jean-Zera Mongé's crew produces results, reflecting his pragmatic mindset regarding their ventures. He is knowledgeable about the dynamics of the local treasure-seeking community and hints at a potential rivalry, underscoring his interests in the events related to Ghost of March of March and the unrest in the city. Giannis is a treasure broker specializing in valuable artifacts and historical items, known for being shrewd and calculating, seeking a substantial payout from the party for their findings. He negotiates terms, offering 15% of his 25% cut upfront for the artifacts presented to him, which can include books, a Malkoç Family Banner, and jewelry.

Moreover, Giannis has hired a group of treasure hunters, indicating that his connections and interests in the area extend beyond simple transactions. He is viewed as an authority in treasure recovery, with references made to his involvement in issues related to undead in the region. Giannis embodies the archetype of a resourceful broker who seeks to profit from the party's endeavors while maintaining a network of contacts within the local underworld.

In addition to his dealings in the black market, Giannis is associated with Osman and linked to a Community's A Certain Community. His name has surfaced in the context of a potential corruption inquiry, suggesting connections to powerful and criminal circles. A significant event saw the carriage route stop at Giannis's house gate, where Jean-Zera was dropped off, indicating a public association that could tarnish Jean-Zera's reputation. The actions of the A Certain Community aimed to publicly link Jean-Zera to Giannis, highlighting the weight Giannis carries in the political landscape. The mention of "Kavalan Pasha," the embattled Egyptian governor, in relation to Giannis suggests his involvement in complex political dynamics that could complicate future dealings, particularly in fencing artifacts.
