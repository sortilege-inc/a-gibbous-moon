The Folk of the City

Hano 'the Widow'

Recently, Hano has become associated with a bounty concerning a selective-targeting undead threat. She has been negotiating terms for this bounty, facilitating deals and transactions in the Undercity, and serving as a point of contact for those seeking to address the undead situation. Her keen interest in a bounty hunter who had succumbed to a strange fever remains, as she revealed that this man, a tailor by trade and a prolific monster hunter, had last been engaged in a dangerous mission against a Vampire Cult. Despite the best efforts of doctors and imams, he remained largely unresponsive, having only transitioned from a total coma to displaying some movement. Hano expressed concern that the hunter's condition might be tied to something beyond mere illness, indicating her awareness of the supernatural elements at play. She granted a group of adventurers the privacy to discuss the situation, demonstrating her willingness to cooperate and facilitate their investigation.

In her role as a patron, Hano commands respect for her expertise in monster hunting. She operates an establishment where she evaluates and compensates adventurers for their trophies, rewarding them with gold and enchanted weapons for their successful encounters. Upon the arrival of a group with the body of an albasti, she expressed strong approval, noting they had exceeded her expectations and rewarded them with 25 gold pieces and a Handaxe +1. Hano instructed them to relay her impressions to Ahra 'the Wolf,' a Janissary leader, showcasing her connections and respect within the community. She also provided practical advice on navigating the Undercity, cautioning against name-dropping her to avoid inflated prices. Her pragmatic demeanor reflects a deep understanding of the challenges faced by adventurers, as well as a wealth of knowledge regarding local lore, including the significance of entities like the Ghost of March.

In addition to her dealings with adventurers, Hano plays a significant role in addressing disturbances at a Cemetery. Her knowledge of supernatural elements is evident, as she expressed an impressed reaction upon receiving a creature's body from Sidra al-Najjar and Ahmet Yusuf. In recognition of their efforts in resolving the issue with the evil spirits, she rewarded them with a Silver Yatagan, a set of 30 Silver-Tipped Throwing Knives, and 200 gold, further indicating her position of authority. When Sidra and Ahmet arrived at her home with the creature's body, Hano confirmed their success and inquired about their use of ranged weapons, showing interest in their capabilities and the context of their recent encounter. Following their discussion on rewards, she expressed satisfaction with their contributions to the situation, reinforcing her role as a key figure in the ongoing battle against the supernatural threats that plague the city.

Hano is also a bounty broker operating in the undercity environment, associated with a network that includes an assistant named Yusuf. During an interaction, she displayed curiosity about a large bundle brought to her, speculating whether it contained an entire body or a remarkably sized head. Hano expressed admiration and confusion upon seeing the size of the bundle. She handed over a bag containing 200 gold and a scroll with two instances of the Exorcise Genie spell to Sidra al-Najjar as payment for a bounty. Her demeanor shows she is accustomed to dealing with unusual and potentially dangerous items, as evidenced by her reaction to the bundle. She mentioned checking her inventory, indicating she manages various bounties and possibly other magical items. Her role as a broker involves the exchange of goods and services related to the underworld, including magical artifacts and information.
