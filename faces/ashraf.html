The Folk of the City

Ashraf

Ashraf is a talking tortoise who serves as the caretaker of Osman Hamdi Bay's house and oversees his library. His ability to speak results from exposure to wild magic, granting him a unique perspective and wisdom. He manages the household in Osman Hamdi Bay's absence and expresses annoyance at disturbances, particularly at night. Ashraf is known for his prickly demeanor but often displays sharp wit in conversations. His brusque nature is further highlighted by his extreme rudeness, which he often directs at those who intrude upon his peace.

During a late-night encounter, he chastised a visitor for making noise, expressing displeasure at being disturbed from his sleep. He emphasized his role in caring for the house and remarked on the curiosity of those who visit. Despite his gruff exterior, Ashraf hints at a deeper understanding of the world around him, adding intrigue to his character and his role in the household.

In addition to his caretaking duties, Ashraf possesses a high level of competence regarding the practical frameworks for fixing spectral races. He demonstrates a deep understanding of the mechanics involved, explaining that fixing a race is easy for one-off hits but riskier for sustained schemes. He advocates for a layered approach to avoid exposure and notes that intermediaries can help reduce the risk of being caught. His lack of patience for gossip reflects a transactional nature in his interactions, further emphasizing his brusque demeanor.

More recently, Ashraf has expressed concern about disturbances in the library and takes offense at implications regarding his responsibilities, clarifying that he does not guard the library but attends to it while present. He wears a string around his neck with a small key attached and has a preference for flowers, particularly enjoying eating them, as well as pears and dates. In an interaction with the party, Ashraf confronted Jean-Zera about his friends being in the library and complained about its state, noting that some books have been taken. He mentioned that the intruders resembled those the party associates with and referred to Osman having recently sought help from “The Shahmeran.” Ashraf reluctantly accepted flowers from Sidra as a distraction while the party investigated the library.
