The Folk of the City

Tree Witch

a spindly, roughly five-foot-tall, ape-like creature, invisible creature, Invisible Attacker

The Invisible Attacker is a spindly creature, approximately five feet tall, resembling an ape. This elusive being possesses the ability to move invisibly, evading detection until it chooses to reveal itself. During a chaotic encounter, it was seen climbing among the branches of a burning tree, its contorted form suggesting the agility and mischievousness typical of fae entities. The creature launched an attack on Sidra al-Najjar and Ahmet Yusuf by hurling rocks from its concealed position.

Sidra, with her enhanced perception, was the first to detect the Invisible Attacker amidst the chaos of the burning tree. After she pointed out its location, Ahmet launched a Fire Bolt at the creature, which reacted with an aggrieved bark and attempted to extinguish the flames that clung to it. Following a coordinated strike in which Ahmet threw a rock, the creature was knocked off balance. Ultimately, it succumbed to a Sleep spell cast by Ahmet, falling from the tree and sustaining lethal damage from the fall. After the creature's demise, Sidra and Ahmet extinguished the fire on the tree and carried the creature's body back to the Scavengers, resolving the immediate threat it posed.

The Invisible Attacker is also an unseen entity mentioned by the Scavengers, known for having taken the tree that housed them. She is connected to a larger tree located two hills over from the Cemetery, where Sidra and Ahmet encountered the Scavengers. The Scavengers express fear and resentment towards the Invisible Attacker, indicating they do not wish to share cups or items with her and characterizing her as malevolent. This suggests her actions have caused them distress by displacing them from their home.

During an investigation at the Cemetery, Sidra and Ahmet discover the Invisible Attacker's impact on the area when the Scavengers mention her in relation to local disturbances. The Scavengers emphasize their refusal to share with the Invisible Attacker, reinforcing her negative reputation. Her name remains unspecified in their discussions, and she is described through the effects of her actions on the Scavengers and the tree she has taken, which is central to the conflict.
