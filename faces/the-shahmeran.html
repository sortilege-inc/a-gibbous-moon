The Shahmeran

Shahmeran

The Shahmeran is bound beneath the Basilica Cistern since the time of Justinian and Theodora. The last fight against her and her spellcasters occurred during that era. Alemdar Mustafa Pasha of March reacted sharply when the name "The Shahmeran" was mentioned, showing a significant level of concern regarding this entity.

A red-dressed prisoner in the same undercity prison once claimed to be the Shahmeran, and the party freed her on that assumption—but she proved an impostor: a sorceress who had merely played along, and who, once loosed, turned on them (transforming Jean-Zera Mongé into a goat and striking with a death-glare) before the party killed her. The true Shahmeran, they learned, remained sealed behind the prison's strongest door.

Behind that great anti-magic door the party at last met the true The Shahmeran: an enormous serpent-woman, twelve feet tall from a vast coil, held taut by runic chains at the four corners of her cell in a wash of pale green light. Imprisoned since Emperor Justinian and Queen Theodora—whose magi had subdued her after she raised great magic from the earth to defend Constantinople against Justinian's conquest—she offered the party aid in exchange for her freedom. The negotiation was perilous: she plunged the chamber into magical darkness, turned Sidra al-Najjar into a lizard, and turned aside their spells so each rebounded on its caster. In the end Miray Ruba Mata Biru and Sidra al-Najjar broke two of her four chains, and she counted the bargain kept. She then decrypted Osman's Journal—a Lodge cipher no other reader had cracked—revealing that Mira, sister of Haseki Sultan Bergüzar Hatun, is a diviner whose living presence mutes the Sultan's full power, and giving up Mira's location. She warned against letting Bergüzar become another Theodora, urged the party to keep Mira alive, and told them they could reach her again by asking Sidra's djinn. She remains only partially freed—two chains broken—beneath the Basilica Cistern.
