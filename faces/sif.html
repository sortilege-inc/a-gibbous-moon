The Folk of the City

Sif

The tiefling proprietor, known as Sif, stands approximately six feet tall and possesses distinct non-human facial features. Initially, Sif has a curt and unfriendly demeanor, displaying rudeness, especially towards newcomers. However, they engage in conversation with clients with whom they have established rapport. Sif operates from a cramped shop named "Sif’s Books and Manuscripts," specializing in the rare book trade within the Undercity.

Sif first interacts with Jean-Zera Mongé, who seeks to negotiate the sale of a book. Initially disinterested, Sif invites Jean-Zera to discuss further after observing their persistence and interest. This interaction signifies a shift from a curt reception to a willingness to engage in dialogue.

Recently, Sif sold a volume to Chique Manu, a wealthy bookmaker, and is recognized for their expertise within the community. In an interaction with Gizem Faruki, Sif described Chique Manu as "chicken," reflecting a negative opinion of the buyer. Sif provided information about the Undercity, noting the Styx Bathhouse and the Misty Pond as notable places for self-care and networking. Sif prefers to keep transactions straightforward and avoids mingling, indicating a transactional approach to interactions.
