Vlad Tepes

Vlad Tepes

Vlad Tepes is a powerful vampire, an imposing undead entity that emerged from a cold mist, stretching his arms and declaring it felt good to be free. He is characterized by a penchant for killing and is identified as a significant threat, often referred to as "the black world" by the Ghost of March, who clarifies that Vlad is not merely a ghost but something far worse. Empowered by blood, Vlad's presence is formidable, and he can conjure floating blades that drip with blood, indicating a deep connection to dark magic.

Bound within the ruins of a church commissioned by his brother centuries ago, Vlad expressed relief at being freed from his cage. The Ghost of March, who has resided in the church for a long time, desires for the party to confront and slay Vlad to free him from his imprisonment, hinting at the extensive history surrounding Vlad's binding and the supernatural events tied to him. This complex relationship between Vlad and the Ghost suggests significant consequences should Vlad be released.

In a tense encounter, Vlad emerged from the mist, flexing his arms and proclaiming his freedom. He swiftly killed Hayriah, a leader of armed men, along with another individual, demonstrating his lethal capabilities and instilling fear and confusion among the party members. The party responded rapidly, particularly Miray, who utilized a radiant Moonbeam that inflicted severe damage on Vlad. Despite his formidable nature, Vlad was unable to withstand the radiant assault, leading to his demise as he was stripped of his armor and evaporated, leaving only his armor clattering to the ground. This destruction significantly impacted the encounter, resolving a looming threat within the dungeon.
