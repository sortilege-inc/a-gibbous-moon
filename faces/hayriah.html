Hayriah

Hayriah, also known as Hayriah, is a notable rival in treasure hunting, particularly in the context of undercity activities linked to Nazif’s Perfumery stall. Dressed in expedition gear reminiscent of classic adventurers, she possesses a commanding presence and authoritative demeanor, which is evident in her interactions with others. Hayriah leads a group of armed men, asserting control and demanding compliance from those she encounters. Her competitive nature is highlighted by her strategic approach to acquiring valuable resources or information, as demonstrated when she underbids another party.

Hayriah's involvement in the city's affairs has created tension, as others seek to outpace her in achieving results. Recognized within the city’s networks, she holds a degree of influence or notoriety. Her activities are connected to ongoing power struggles and competition, particularly in the aftermath of the Ghost of March's actions.

When confronted by Sidra al-Najjar and her companions, Hayriah seeks to ascertain their identities and intentions, insisting they return any items taken from the site. She attempts to divide the party by ordering her men to escort them into a larger room while she prepares to clear the area. Showing a willingness to engage in combat, Hayriah advances toward Sidra with a staff charged with crackling purple energy, resembling an Eldritch Blast effect. In a display of her magical prowess, she forces Jean-Zera Mongé to drop his weapon.

Recently, Hayriah met her end after stepping into a Moonbeam conjured by Miray, resulting in catastrophic damage that killed her. After her death, her body was further harmed by a vampire that manifested shortly after. Among her belongings, a quarterstaff and a satchel containing a valuable memoir attributed to Mehmed II were discovered. Following her demise, a letter from Jean-Zera Mongé referenced that Hayriah is "no longer in the picture," suggesting that her involvement in a treasure job has concluded and positioning Jean-Zera as the sole source of valuable information, indicating her notable reputation within the treasure hunting community may still influence competition among treasure hunters, even in her absence.
