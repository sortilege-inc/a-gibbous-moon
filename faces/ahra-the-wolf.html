The Folk of the City

Ahra 'the Wolf'

Ahra "the Wolf," a sturdily built woman clad in the distinctive garb of the Janissaries, stands as a formidable leader among her faction, embodying the remnants of a once-great military order. Her reputation precedes her, marked by a strong presence and a commitment to restoring the Janissaries' former glory while protecting the populace of the Ottoman Empire from those who would exploit them. She maintains a formal alliance with Osman Hamdi Bey, receiving sealed letters that signify their ongoing communication.

When the party first encounters Ahra, she acknowledges the letter from Osman and expresses her willingness to assist them in their quest to recover his stolen journal. However, her support comes with conditions; she requires compensation and tasks the party with infiltrating various criminal enterprises to identify key figures in the underworld, including Giannis "the Cut," Hano "the Widow," and Chique Manu. Ahra's pragmatic and direct approach underscores the seriousness of their mission and the dangers that lie ahead.

Though not always physically present, Ahra's influence is felt throughout the Janissaries. Her sharp-eyed assessment of situations and individuals is well-known, particularly regarding the capabilities of her recruits. Hano "the Widow," a member of her faction, often speaks of her approval of new recruits, highlighting her respected position of authority within the community.

The Janissaries face a significant challenge when they encounter an albasti, a dream-feeding entity that preys on the minds of the sleeping. Demonstrating bravery and resourcefulness, they confront this threat and ultimately bring the creature's body to Hano "the Widow." His strong approval of their actions is accompanied by a mention of Ahra's positive assessment of their recent efforts, further solidifying her role as a key figure in overseeing their monster-hunting activities and reinforcing her status as a leader committed to the safety and strength of her people.

Ahra "the Wolf" holds a position of influence in the city's political and social dynamics. She is closely associated with Osman Hamdi Bey and is actively engaged in matters concerning the recent unrest in the city. Managing the fallout from violent events, including the actions of the Ghost of March, who has been implicated in the death of Nazif, a perfumist, she remains vigilant.

In a meeting with a group, Ahra acknowledges the turmoil and confirms her knowledge of the Ghost of March's actions. She assigns the group the task of dealing with the undead at Saint Benoit Church as compensation for a journal they recovered. Expressing concern about the situation, she notes that several state armories were broken into and comments on the bravery of a protest that did not affect her people. Ahra also provides information about a searched residence of an outspoken protester, indicating her engagement in community safety and political intrigue. Mentioning the term "meftun," referring to the undead troubling Saint Benoit, she advises the group on navigating the undercity routes, demonstrating her extensive knowledge of the city's complexities.

Recently, Ahra has become increasingly concerned about undead activities. She expressed gratitude towards the party for resolving a situation involving the undead at a site of concern and informed them that she has returned a journal to Osman, who wishes to speak with them. Ahra has permitted the party to keep or sell the spoils collected during their expedition, showing urgency in resolving the undead issue while emphasizing the need for resolution. Her demeanor suggests authority and responsibility as she coordinates information between the party and Osman, fulfilling her role as a liaison in matters concerning the undead.

In addition to her ongoing efforts, Ahra "the Wolf" is involved in forming a united front with Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, the leader of the Ghosts. Alemdar acknowledges past poor relations with Ahra "the Wolf" and seeks reconciliation, indicating her importance in the current political landscape. There are concerns regarding her perspective on the inclusion of diverse expressions of humanity in defending the Empire, which Alemdar believes should be broadened. Furthermore, Ahra "the Wolf" is linked to recent events involving weapons caches being targeted around the time of the Festival of Candles, suggesting a connection to ongoing conflicts within the Empire.

In the latest developments, it has come to light that an assassin known as Ahra 'the Wolf', associated with the Janissaries, is implicated in the killing of Alexander's wife, Nadia, due to her use of magic. This act has incited a vendetta against her from Alexander, who seeks retribution. During an encounter in the catacombs, Alexander reveals his past with Ahra 'the Wolf', expressing his anger and desire for revenge, and warns the party about her lethal tendencies, emphasizing the perilous nature of their mission amid the dangerous dynamics surrounding her and the Janissaries.
