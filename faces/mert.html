The Folk of the City

Mert

Mert, often referred to as Papa Mert, is an esteemed alchemist in the Undercity, known for his brusque manner and commanding presence. He operates within a market setting, interacting with various characters, including Jean-Zera Mongé, who establishes rapport with him through respectful persistence. Although Mert's initial demeanor is curt and unfriendly, he becomes more open to conversation after being approached by Jean-Zera, suggesting he possesses valuable knowledge for those who earn his respect.

Residing in a middle to upper-class area of the city, Mert serves not only as a contact for the party, indicating his role as a resource for alchemical needs or information, but also as a mentor to aspiring alchemists like Sidra al-Najjar. He allows others to observe his work, but under the strict condition of silence and without hands-on participation, reinforcing his authority and expertise in the field. This instructional approach cultivates a learning atmosphere where knowledge is imparted indirectly, further establishing Papa Mert's reputation as a knowledgeable figure in alchemy.

In addition to his alchemical prowess, Papa Mert is recognized as an apothecary and mentor, particularly noted for his expertise in the medicinal and toxic properties of various plants and fungi. He instructs others in safe collection methods, emphasizing the importance of a white powder called "Biyin," which absorbs toxins from the skin. In a notable interaction with Sidra al-Najjar, he identified a dangerous fungus she had collected and insisted she scrub her hands with Biyin to remove any toxins. He provided guidance on the basic properties of the samples she brought, showcasing his role as a teacher and a resource for those navigating the complexities of the Undercity's flora.

Recently, Papa Mert demonstrated his expertise in compounding remedies when Sidra al-Najjar sought his assistance for a young tiefling suffering from blood rot. Despite his gruff demeanor, he showed underlying compassion, listening carefully to her concerns and offering to guide her through the process of creating a remedy. He provided a thick whitish-blue paste as part of the treatment and explained that its application required a ritual component, highlighting his knowledge of both alchemical and magical practices. During the application, he assisted Sidra by uncovering the affected areas and overseeing the ritual. After the treatment, he expressed gratitude to Sidra for her help, acknowledging that it was his first practical use of the technique, before departing to return home.

In addition to his alchemical expertise, Papa Mert is a fixer with local ties, known for providing temporary lodging and assistance to those in need. He possesses a disguise kit and shares a cloak, demonstrating his resourcefulness. Familiar with the Undercity and its inhabitants, he often questions newcomers about their needs and intentions, offering shelter for three days, which indicates a level of trust and familiarity with those he aids. Papa Mert is involved in the logistics of the party's operations, ensuring they have a safe place to regroup and plan their next moves. His presence in the Undercity contributes to the diverse personalities encountered there, and his commanding demeanor ensures that those who wish to learn from him must respect his methods and the silence he demands.
