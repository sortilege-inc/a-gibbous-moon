The Folk of the City

Mosolite Nazif

Nazif was a tall man, standing above six feet, got up in a loose shirt laced at the throat after the manner of a shoelace. He kept a prosperous perfume shop, Nazif's Scents by name, in the Grand Bazaar, a place ostentatious in its fittings and drawing a clientele of every description, where the whole culture of the crafting and the selling of scents was carried on with no little ceremony.

Amidst the bustle of a festival Nazif fell to talking business, fretful for his trade and in converse with his fellow merchants, Chique Manu chief among them. His day took a black turn, however, when the Ghost of March confronted him — an imposing figure clad in an old Imperial uniform and bearing a whip of tempered steel. The Ghost of March charged him with grinding down his own people beneath the weight of tributes, at which Nazif visibly paled, the fear washing over him as he knew the Ghost of March for a master to be obeyed; and in one swift and brutal motion the whip lashed out, ensnared him, and snapped his neck.

Nazif was a perfumist, then, slain by the Ghost of March and his companions at the Festival of Candles. He had been seated beside Chique Manu when the charge of betrayal was laid against him, his own offices having been supplanted by the Council of the Ninth Life. Gizem Faruki confirmed him dead, coming upon the body in the seating-area whilst Chique Manu stood by in his shock; and the killing prompted the Bostanji Imperial Guard to declare a state of emergency and to set about scattering the crowd, so kindling much talk of what it must mean for the balance of power in the city.

There was, moreover, an occasion when Gizem approached him in search of passage to the Undercity, and Nazif confirmed that he could arrange the thing, though not without a toll; he took a toll coin of Sidra to cover her entry, and led the party to an old stone door that gave onto well-lit stairs descending to a carpeted hall of stone, and thence to the thronging marketplace of the Undercity. After his death the company set about examining his perfumery stall in the Grand Bazaar, the better to learn who now held the keys to the Undercity's approaches — and there it came to light that Nazif had been relieved of those duties by the Ghost of March, a token of no small shift in the Undercity's order of power.
