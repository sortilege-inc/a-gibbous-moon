Mosolite Nazif

Nazif is a tall individual, standing over six feet, characterized by his loose-fitting shirt tied at the top in a shoelace style. He operates a prosperous perfume shop known as Nazif's Scents, located in the Grand Bazaar, which attracts a diverse clientele. The shop is ostentatious and emphasizes a strong culture surrounding the crafting and selling of perfumes.

During a bustling festival, Nazif finds himself engaged in business discussions, concerned about his operations and interacting with other merchants, notably Chique Manu. However, his day takes a dark turn when he is confronted by the Ghost of March, an imposing figure clad in an old Imperial uniform and wielding a tempered steel whip. The Ghost of March accuses Nazif of harming his people through tributes, causing Nazif to visibly pale, fear washing over him as he recognizes Ghost of March's authority. In a swift and brutal act, the Ghost of March lashes out with the whip, ensnaring Nazif and snapping his neck.

Nazif was a perfumist who was killed by the Ghost of March and his companions during the Festival of Candles. He sat beside Chique Manu when he was accused of betrayal related to his responsibilities being supplanted by the Council of the Nine Lives. His body was confirmed dead by Gizem Faruki, who found him in the seating area while Chique Manu remained in shock nearby. The assassination prompted a state of emergency declared by the Bostanji Imperial Guard as they attempted to disperse the crowd, sparking discussions regarding the implications for power dynamics in the city.

Later, when Gizem approaches him seeking passage to the Undercity, Nazif confirms he can arrange it, indicating there will be a toll associated with the passage. He accepts a toll coin from Sidra to cover her entry fee into the Undercity. Nazif then leads them to an old stone door that opens to well-lit stairs descending into a carpeted stone hall, ultimately leading to the bustling Undercity marketplace. Following his death, the party sought to investigate Nazif’s perfumery stall in the Grand Bazaar to understand who would control undercity access, revealing that Nazif had been relieved of his duties by the Ghost of March, indicating a significant shift in the undercity's power structure.
