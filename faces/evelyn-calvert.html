The Folk of the City

Evelyn Calvert

Evelyn Calvert is of the Calvert household, seated at Thymbra Farm to the north of Constantinople. She is wife to Frederick Calvert, and together they keep a table for guests of standing, Osman Hamdi Bey and General Patrice de MacMahon among them.

Evelyn steers a conversation through matters of war and of trade with a practised hand, and her sway over a gathering is plain to see. At a dinner in the farmhouse she moved easily among the guests, governing the humours of the table and keeping the talk in flow; set beside Sophia, a younger guest less sure of herself in company, her ease was the more marked — smooth and assured, and altogether at home in the society of the evening.
