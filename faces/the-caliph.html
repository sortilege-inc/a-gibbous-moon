The Caliph

The Caliph was a significant political figure in the Ottoman Empire whose recent death has created a power vacuum and shifted leadership dynamics. His passing is confirmed in a conversation between Crown Prince Abd-ul Mejid and Osman Hamdi Bey, where Osman Hamdi Bey agrees to assist the Crown Prince in the transition of power. The Caliph's legacy influences the political landscape of the Empire and affects the actions of various characters.

The party learns about The Caliph's death through discussions with the Crown Prince and Osman Hamdi Bey, who emphasize the urgency of the situation. Osman Hamdi Bey acknowledges the need for assistance as he navigates the responsibilities that arise from the Crown Prince's ascension. The group recognizes that they are being drawn into the political machinations following The Caliph's demise. Osman Hamdi Bey outlines a plan to utilize their skills to address emerging threats to the Empire. The situation sets the stage for the party's involvement in clandestine operations under the "certain Community," the Empire's secret service, amid the tension surrounding The Caliph's death.
