Alemdar Mustafa Pasha

Alemdar Mustafa Pasha is a bandaged leader, showing only his eyes, and is reputedly the dead commander of the Sekban. He is characterized by his tactical demeanor and emotional detachment, emphasizing readiness for potential bloodshed. His hands are tightly wrapped in magical bandages that preserve him and protect those around him. Alemdar is associated with the Sekban and is noted for bridging the worlds above and below.

During a meeting with the party, Alemdar greeted Gizem Faruki by name, indicating foreknowledge of their arrival. He expressed approval of their deeds as reported by Sharif the Strong Fur, a cat liaison. Alemdar inquired if they had been followed and discussed the need for a united front with Ahra 'the Wolf' of the Janissaries, despite their prior enmity. He requested that they offer terms to her, emphasizing the importance of unity for the Empire and all its occupants. Alemdar mentioned a young tiefling named Shehrazat, who was suffering from blood rot, and asked Sidra al-Najjar to visit and help her if possible. He offered a leather-tied journal of Osman's, which he had taken but could not decipher, to the party for their use.

Alemdar's office contained a fine, battle-worthy longsword behind his desk. He maintained a posture of disinformation, advising the group to keep the appearance of eternal Janissary–Sekban enmity for the benefit of others’ misperceptions. He invited referrals for recruits and those versed in elements or alchemy to join the “new Sekban Army.”

The Alemdar Mustafa Pasha is also recognized as a winged entity, a supernatural being that embodies both beauty and dread. This powerful figure resembles a humanoid woman, distinguished by spindly limbs and ambiguous hair that contribute to her unsettling presence. Identified as an albasti, she is a dream-feeding entity that preys on the vulnerable, particularly those in a deep slumber. Her face is marked by a vent-like flesh grille, devoid of traditional features such as a nose and mouth, further enhancing her eerie appearance.

Within the hidden depths of the Undercity, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha is an influential figure, revered for maintaining order among its residents and ensuring the safety of children from the predatory threats posed by various gangs. Her name echoes through the streets, recalled with familiarity and respect, indicating her significant role in the community. The Alemdar Mustafa Pasha is associated with the Sekban soldiers, known for their historical role in dismantling the Janissaries, and appears dressed in uniforms reminiscent of this disbanded military unit. She controls a network of interconnected buildings in the Undercity, showcasing her organizational prowess.

In a recent encounter, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha manifested in a dimly lit alleyway, where she took the hand of a human accomplice while stalking a potential victim. The atmosphere was thick with supernatural dread, rendering nearby observers powerless to intervene. With a swift motion, she attempted to induce sleep in Ahmet by passing her hand over his eyes, seeking to draw him into a dream state. However, her predatory actions sparked a chaotic confrontation. Ahmet and his allies fought back fiercely, ultimately incapacitating the albasti in a struggle that echoed with the urgency of her dark intentions.

Earlier, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha had issued a warning to Rufus Dimitrious, urging him to "seek out the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha" as she fled past him, hinting at her ability to manipulate perception and engage in magical actions. During her performance, her song was noted to weave a Darkness spell, showcasing her arcane connections. Now, Kore has informed the party that she has communicated with the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha to arrange safe lodging for a group of children previously under the control of a gang leader named Mr. Karen. This arrangement, requiring a fee of 20 gold that the party acquires from a defeated dwarf, underscores her status as a protector and provider in the Undercity. Under the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha's influence, the children receive vital support and protection, further cementing her reputation as a legitimate authority figure amid the chaos represented by the gangs.

Following the battle with Ahmet, the group assessed the inert body of the albasti, securing it for transport to a local patron for appraisal, marking a pivotal moment in their ongoing journey intertwined with the enigmatic yet protective presence of the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha.

In the aftermath of her actions, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha executed Nazif, a perfumist, accusing him of betrayal and claiming that his responsibilities were being supplanted by the Council of the Nine Lives. This act led to a state of emergency declared by the Bostanji Imperial Guard, prompting a chaotic dispersal of the crowd. Following the assassination, Sidra al-Najjar approached the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, requesting an audience. She responded, “Now is not the time, little one, but soon,” before leading her entourage away as guards arrived to restore order. Her group then swiftly melted into the shadows of the city. Gizem Faruki later noted that the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha’s followers wore Sekban uniforms, linking them to a history of violent conflict. The Alemdar Mustafa Pasha was later reported to have relieved Nazif of his duties, further entrenching her influence in the Undercity. Her actions sparked discussions about unrest in the city, including weapon thefts and protests, with the party tasked to investigate the unrest surrounding the ruins of Saint Benoit Church, where the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha's activities were impacting the state of the unquiet dead.

Recently, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha has been linked to a journal that Osman claims is a fake, leading to a meeting in the Undercity at the Reef Tavern to obtain the true journal. This location is familiar to the party, and Osman displays hesitance about entering the Undercity, implying that the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha may be dangerous and possibly manipulative. The party contemplates the implications of aligning with Osman and the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, suggesting that her presence is shrouded in mystery, representing a blend of past grievances and future machinations tied to the narrative within the Undercity. Furthermore, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha's brother, Radu the Handsome, expressed gratitude towards the party for freeing and destroying him, indicating that the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha was previously bound or imprisoned. Upon her release, she conveyed that her burden is lifted and she will depart, suggesting a conclusion to a past entanglement.

Additionally, the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha has been mentioned in relation to a sinking ship, where a fleeing individual referenced her, indicating her involvement in significant events. Her operations may intersect with various factions, including rival treasure hunters and A Certain Community's A Certain Community, which opposes pluralism and seeks to suppress magical groups. The motivations and goals of the Alemdar Mustafa Pasha are of increasing interest to those navigating interactions with this enigmatic figure.

Alemdar Pasha is also a senior commander in the Turkish military, known for his martial prowess and philosophical teachings. He is associated with a youthful version of Padishah Abd-ul Mejid, the future Padisha, and emphasizes the importance of discipline and enlightenment through physical training. Alemdar Pasha teaches that wounds bring enlightenment and embodies universal ecstasy through disciplined precision. He reprimands Padishah Abd-ul Mejid for using magic against their own people, demonstrating a strict adherence to principles of conduct in combat.

In a shared dream sequence, Alemdar Pasha instructs the young Padishah Abd-ul Mejid on the significance of focus and the consequences of distraction. He demonstrates authority and wisdom, countering a magical attempt by Padishah Abd-ul Mejid, showcasing his adeptness in both magic and martial skills.

Alemdar Mustafa Pasha has been involved in the party's affairs, sending them to find Alexander and expressing concern for Osman Hamdi Bey's welfare.
