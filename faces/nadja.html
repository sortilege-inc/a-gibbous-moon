The Folk of the City

Nadja

Nadja is a woman in her thirties, known for her self-assured presence as she manages the kitchen at a mosque's soup kitchen in Constantinople. With a practical and community-oriented demeanor, she oversees volunteers, coordinates meal preparations, and engages warmly with both children and adults in the community. Her approachability is evident as she assists those in need, distributing food and offering shelter after meals, yet she remains cautious, expressing skepticism toward unsolicited offers from adults.

During a meal service, Nadja takes the opportunity to involve party members in her work, asking about a man who was last seen at the soup line a few days prior. Her commitment to the community is further highlighted by her interest in theological discussions, particularly regarding the inclusivity of Islamic practices for magically arisen non-humans. Engaging in thoughtful conversations with Gizem Faruki, she explores the spiritual implications of faith for diverse beings and shares concerns about a friend seeking guidance on these matters.

Nadja's proactive nature shines through as she suggests meeting Kore, a being residing in the Undercity, to discuss spiritual communication. Her familiarity with local lore is evident when she mentions Nazif's shop as a gateway to the Undercity, showcasing her role as a bridge for individuals seeking understanding of faith. Through her empathetic approach, Nadja not only fulfills her responsibilities at the soup kitchen but also facilitates meaningful discussions around spirituality and inclusivity within her community.
