The Folk of the City

unfamiliar soldier

A soldier with East Asian facial features appears in a waterborne mirror, wearing a fur cap and a bow strapped to his back. He is visible in a large mirror set just below the water’s surface, looking up toward the party. When Gizem waved, the soldier removed the bow from his back and observed cautiously, offering no spoken reply or communication through the water and glass. Miray later identified his appearance as resembling Mongol-era warriors. After Gizem tossed baklava toward the mirror, something thrashed below, briefly obscuring him before the water settled.
