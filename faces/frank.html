The Folk of the City

Frank

Frank Calvert, a courteous and engaging host from Thymbra Farm, located north of Constantinople, recently organized a dinner attended by notable figures such as Osman Hamdi Bay and General Patrice de MacMahon. As the brother of Frederick Calvert, Frank Calvert actively participated in discussions surrounding local archaeological excavations of an ancient Greek site, reflecting his keen interest in the affairs of the dig. During the dinner, he introduced Rufus Dimitrious, a trusted coastal guide, while discreetly concealing Rufus's involvement in smuggling logistics. Frank Calvert privately compensated Rufus with 50 gold for his services in arranging smuggling operations and identifying suitable coastal coves.

In the midst of these social interactions, Frank Calvert found himself embroiled in a harrowing incident at a farmhouse near Constantinople and the adjacent dig site. Alongside Heinrich, he became trapped within a luminous ward, facing a large square door adorned with a design of Aphrodite and snake-handled pulls featuring jeweled eyes. Upon regaining movement, Frank Calvert was horrified by the experience, reeling and ultimately succumbing to nausea. Despite his shaken state, he expressed a strong desire to explore the mysterious door, insisting it led to ancient Troy and urging others to attempt to open it again.

However, panic set in when Heinrich was taken by a serpent, prompting Frank Calvert to call for help in a moment of desperation. His inability to speak Turkish further complicated his understanding of the unfolding situation. After the incident, he followed Heinrich back to the farmhouse, displaying skepticism and reluctance to explore the site further without a proper plan. Frank Calvert's character emerged as somewhat impulsive, eager for discovery and treasure, yet visibly shaken by the terrifying events he had just endured.
