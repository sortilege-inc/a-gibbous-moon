The Folk of the City

Susanna

Susanna is an elven woman who appears youthful, likely in her early twenties. She is involved in activities related to spectral horses, specifically performing a necrotic debilitation on one of the Green spectral horses during a clandestine moment. She is seen whispering to Chique Manu, a bookmaker, indicating a potential collusion in the context of chariot races. During a race at the Hippodrome, she is observed by Sidra al-Najjar, who hides in the shadows to avoid detection. Sidra relays information about Susanna's actions to her companions, indicating that Susanna is engaged in something secretive and possibly harmful to the spectral horses. Jean-Zera Mongé seeks an introduction to Susanna after the race, which Chique Manu agrees to arrange.

More recently, Susanna has been identified as a fixer associated with the racing scene, present at the Spectral Hippodrome. She engages with Jean-Zera Mongé, expressing skepticism about the promises made by Chique Manu and advising Jean-Zera not to interfere with the races. Susanna chuckles and purses her lips while speaking. When Jean-Zera requests time with a faceless ghost, Susanna acknowledges the difficulty of obtaining information from the ghostly riders, who are not particularly communicative. She emphasizes the need for Jean-Zera to refrain from interference, indicating her protectiveness of the racing dynamics. After their conversation, she offers Jean-Zera a job, stating that Chique Manu will be in touch to arrange a new race.

In a separate discovery, the remains of an elven female skeleton, identified as Susanna, were found alongside a human male skeleton in a cell. The remains were accompanied by a spellbook, a note, and two healing potions. Examination of the note and surrounding evidence identified them as lovers from Justinian’s time, with the human having worked on a spell intended to save Susanna’s unnatural physiology. The recovered spellbook contained spells such as Disguise Self, Shield, Magic Missile, and Silent Image.
