Haseki Sultan Bergüzar Hatun

Padishah’s Consort

Haseki Sultan Bergüzar Hatun is the wife of the new Abd-ul Mejid, indicating her high status and importance within the political landscape of the Ottoman Empire. Her movements are constrained, suggesting limited freedom compared to others, which is a reason for Osman meeting with her privately. Osman has been meeting with Bergüzar in her quarters, a practice consistent with her status. While her appearance and personal attributes are not detailed, her association with Osman implies a significant role in the political dynamics of the Empire. Conversations surrounding her suggest she may have connections or influence, though specific details about her abilities or intentions are not provided.

Recently, it has been revealed that Bergüzar is the newly crowned Sultan, seated in state alongside her husband, the Padisha. She possesses a youthful appearance, recognized by the trio from their shared dreams. During an encounter, she presented a silver box containing a ribbon-tied scroll to Sublime Porte, intended to erase any memory of their meeting from the mind of an assassin. The Sultan is a formidable magic-user, influencing memories and potentially involved in the dreams experienced by the characters. Although she remained silent during the meeting, Aziz, an official allied with Osman Hamdi Bey, mediated the discussion. She is referred to as a power behind the throne, raising concerns among the group regarding her influence and the implications of her magical abilities. The scroll she provided functions like a powerful, lasting Modify Memory effect, capable of permanently removing recollections.

Bergüzar possesses a memory-erasing scroll, which she used to erase Hamit the Quartz of Damascus's memories after his capture. This scroll contains the same spell that was previously cast on Osman Hamdi Bey. At Yedikule Fortress, the Sublime Porte devised a plan to protect the Vizier-i Aezam by using him as bait to capture Hamit alive. Following Hamit's capture, they utilized Bergüzar's memory scroll to erase his memories of their encounter.

Sultan Bergüzar Hatun is known for her affectionate relationship with her husband, which may influence her political dealings. Her role intertwines with the dynamics of power and influence, particularly concerning her husband's position. Sidra al-Najjar has expressed curiosity about Sultan Bergüzar Hatun, specifically regarding how women influence their husbands in matters of power. Sultan Bergüzar Hatun may play a significant role in the political machinations of the court, potentially leveraging her relationship to affect decisions and alliances.

Jean-Zera Mongé has a significant connection to the Haseki Sultan Bergüzar Hatun. He possesses magical abilities, utilizing spells to engage with supernatural forces, including using magic to assault shadowy foes during a confrontation with semi-corporeal janissary soldiers. Jean-Zera actively participates in discussions and investigations regarding Osman Hamdi Bey and the implications of the Sultan’s sister, a powerful diviner. He collaborates with other characters to gather information and strategize, including approaching the Ghost of March as an ally. His actions include sketching a sigil from memory, which is tied to the mystery involving the consort and her sister. In combat, he employs chill-inducing spectral force and enchanted bolts to combat shadows, demonstrating his proficiency in magic.
