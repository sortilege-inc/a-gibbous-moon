Sophia Schliemann

Sophia Schliemann, a younger woman newly married to Heinrich, finds herself intertwined with the archaeological dig near Thymbra Farm. With a gentle demeanor that belies her inexperience in social matters, she navigates the complexities of her new life, often feeling overwhelmed in discussions about significant topics like trade and war. During a dinner at Thymbra Farm, she engages with Evelyn Calvert, showing a keen interest in her insights, though her lack of confidence is evident as she struggles to keep pace with the seasoned socialites around her.

The following morning, Sophia Schliemann's curiosity leads her to the terrace, where she observes Gizem Faruki practicing Sufi turning. Eager to learn, she asks questions about the intricate steps involved, showcasing her desire to understand and appreciate different cultural practices.

However, her gentle nature is tinged with concern, particularly for Heinrich, who has gone missing. In a moment of urgency, she approaches Osman Hamdi Bey, voicing her worries and advocating for a search party to find him. Her active involvement in the group's discussions about the missing persons reflects her awareness of their collective situation and the potential dangers they face. Sophia Schliemann emerges as a supportive and responsible member of the party, contributing thoughtfully to their decision-making regarding exploration and safety, all while grappling with the weight of her new responsibilities and the uncertainties that lie ahead.
