Night 9 · Session 9 · 2026-01-11

A Certain Meeting

Summary

The party shared a haunting dream featuring a little girl fleeing after her mother killed her father, leaving a sister distraught. This unsettling vision influenced their discussions as they recapped previous encounters and strategized for upcoming challenges. They confirmed that Osman possessed a fake version of his journal, reinforcing their decision to leave him behind. Osman had provided a lead on Matilda Manukian, a half-elf with access to the Undercity, which they planned to follow.

They reviewed the aftermath of their encounter with a vampire, recalling that rival treasure hunters had met their demise at the creature's hands. Some of these hunters may have been slain by the party earlier, and they noted they had never actually entered the church during that prior job. The presence of soldiers seen with the Ghost of March was discussed, identified as the Sekbans, a disbanded group, suggesting their appearance was more symbolic than indicative of true veterans.

As they allocated their recent loot, they divided two uncursed magical short swords, with Jean-Zera and Sidra each claiming one, while retaining grenades and holy water for future encounters. Gizem checked in at her bakery, catching up with regulars and listening for gossip. She learned of a new underground experience at the Hippodrome, which piqued her interest. Osman invited her to an upcoming race, warning her that the horses were not real, and they set a meeting point at the Theodosius Obelisk near the Hagia Sophia.

Gizem attempted to eavesdrop at the hammam for news on Janissaries or ghosts, but patrons quickly shut down such discussions. Meanwhile, Jean-Zera utilized Osman’s library, where staff reported that Osman had not returned due to pressing palace matters. The house librarian revealed that Osman was meeting with Bergüzar, the wife of the new Abd-ul Mejid, in her quarters.

Researching Osman’s artifacts, Jean-Zera discovered that most recovered items were historically significant and financially valuable, tied to Church history and the lineage of Vlad and Radu’s father. A letter arrived requesting an update on their treasure job, prompting Jean-Zera to draft a careful reply that concealed supernatural elements. He sent a deceptive letter to Giannis, indicating success and stating that rival treasure hunter Hayriah was “no longer in the picture,” along with a sketch of the karabela saber to demonstrate value.

Sidra surveyed the Undercity, mapping out residential pockets, an entertainment hub around the Leaky Pit, and frequently traveled routes. She identified the Leaky Pit as a vibrant arts district and noted various amenities, including the Styx Bathhouse and the Thieves Bazaar. During her explorations, she collected hazardous samples of moss and fungi from Misty Pond and received instruction from Papa Mert on the medicinal and toxic properties of the fungi.

As the party prepared to approach the Ghost of March, they agreed on a direct diplomatic strategy, stating their goals and asking what the Ghost desired. They planned to search for Matilda Manukian near the Hagia Sophia, employing a cover of delivering baked goods to shops while Sidra kept watch at a tea shop with Jean-Zera posing as her art instructor. Their plan took an unexpected turn when well-dressed agents approached Jean-Zera, pressing a concealed firearm into his ribs and insisting he accompany them.

In a tense moment, Sidra cast a spell to coordinate with their allies. Using her charm, she reframed the threat posed by the lead agent, shifting his perception of them from complicit deceivers to naive bystanders. Jean-Zera seized the opportunity to cast a spell, compelling the agent to flee and creating a gap in their control. Despite the disruption, Jean-Zera was guided into a waiting carriage with an older man of authority, recognized as the head of a certain community’s secret service.

The official urged Jean-Zera to influence Osman to cease “ecumenical flights,” emphasizing the need for a singular voice within the Empire. He demanded secrecy regarding their conversation, asserting that the service would obtain any necessary information regardless. As the carriage made its way through the city, it stopped at Giannis’s house gate, a calculated move to publicly link Jean-Zera to Giannis. After a brief interaction with a guard, the official re-entered the carriage, leaving Jean-Zera to disembark warily.

Meanwhile, Miray Ruba Mata Biru, in her wild-shaped form, attempted to shadow the carriage via rooftops but botched a leap, ultimately regaining her position to continue following. Gizem’s attempt at a shortcut was blocked by a street festival, but she pushed through to converge with Sidra while keeping an eye on the carriage. As the agents signaled for reinforcements, Sidra and Gizem used a spell to evade their pursuers while maintaining visual contact with the carriage.

Matilda Manukian made contact with Gizem, offering safe passage for the group through a hidden stairway leading to the Leaky Pit in the Undercity. After delivering directions, Matilda departed for other matters, leaving the party to reflect on the implications of their encounter with the agents and the community’s opposition to pluralism. They weighed their options, ultimately deciding to proceed directly to seek the Ghost of March.
