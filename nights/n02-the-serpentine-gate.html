Night 2 · Session 2 · 2025-09-28

The Serpentine Gate

Summary

In the morning at the farmhouse near the dig site outside Constantinople, a loud crack echoed through the air, prompting the curious ten-year-old Ahmet Yusuf to investigate the nearby Thymbra Farm. Upon reaching the tree, he discovered a 30-foot radius of ground that had been completely leached of color, though the tree itself remained alive. Inside the farmhouse, Jean-Zera Mongé briefly sensed a faint magical residue from one of the bedrooms but chose not to pursue it at that moment.

As the group gathered, Rufus Dimitrious encouraged General Patrice de MacMahon to touch the leached tree. The general felt no immediate effects, but the noise startled Osman Hamdi Bey, who began writing furiously in a ciphered journal. Concerned for the missing Heinrich Schliemann, Sophia Schliemann informed Osman, who then tasked the party with searching for Heinrich and possibly Frank Calvert. Bored after examining the tree, Ahmet ventured toward the dig area, inadvertently trampling potential traces.

Rufus shared overheard information about “a hill and a key,” suggesting a connection to the largest nearby hill. As the others set out, Jean-Zera returned to the farmhouse under the pretense of fetching cartographer’s tools, with Rufus accompanying him. Inside a locked study, Rufus successfully picked the lock, revealing shelves filled with classic literature and a marble bust of Homer. Jean-Zera examined the contents, discovering extensive research on Troy and a hidden scroll titled "The Lion’s Key," which appeared to be a spell for opening locked doors. They also found a spare key in the desk before leaving the study unlocked.

On their way to the dig site, Rufus spotted a narrow crevice leading into a cave and called the others to investigate. Inside the partially collapsed chamber, they discovered ancient wall paintings, with Sidra al-Najjar recognizing their antiquity and Jean-Zera concluding their significance. Ahmet discreetly pocketed a decorated pottery shard while the group moved into a larger hall, where they encountered a serpent that emerged from a dark hole. Gizem Faruki diplomatically requested hospitality from the serpent, which agreed to forbear if they removed “interlopers” from the next chamber.

In the adjoining chamber, they found Frank Calvert and Heinrich Schliemann frozen within a luminous ward, facing an Ophidian Door adorned with imagery of Aphrodite. After some time, they managed to release Frank and Heinrich from the ward, but Heinrich insisted on reopening the door immediately, disregarding safety concerns. As tensions rose, the serpent intervened, coiling Heinrich and dragging him away, prompting panic from Frank. While the serpent took Heinrich, Rufus descended into the hole and discovered a nest containing valuables, including a bronze goblet, an incomplete Ouroboros necklace, and a wide triangular arrowhead — an “ankle-biter” that lends venom to any shot fired with it.

Gizem and Frank followed the serpent’s route to an outdoor exit, where they found Heinrich unconscious but alive. Meanwhile, Jean-Zera disrupted the ward by kicking dust through it and sketched the door for future reference. After some debate, Heinrich reluctantly agreed to postpone their exploration of the door and return later with a proper team. Before leaving, Jean-Zera made a clean copy of "The Lion’s Key" and returned the original to the study.

Back at the farmhouse, Heinrich loudly claimed the discovery of ancient Troy, while Osman reminded everyone that exporting artifacts from the Ottoman Empire was a serious offense. Osman received an urgent message requiring his immediate return to Constantinople that evening and requested Jean-Zera’s assistance, along with Rufus, Ahmet, and Sidra. Osman purchased the bronze goblet, the Ouroboros necklace, and ancient coins from Rufus for a total of 80 silver, while Rufus kept the owl brooch and the ankle-biter arrowhead.

Later, aboard a non-government steamer, the group settled into their quarters. Osman played chess with a Pasha, while Sidra and Jean-Zera observed his strategic moves. Rufus reconnected with a fellow smuggler, the Greek Captain, who warned him about Giannis the Cut, a black-market fixer in Constantinople. During the voyage, Sidra sensed that the band’s singer was not entirely human and was weaving a Darkness spell into her lyrics.

An urgent call for help arose when a steward asked Osman to assist a woman in distress on the sleeping deck. Osman, Gizem, and Jean-Zera entered the cabin, while Rufus waited outside. Inside, the “woman” transformed into a wicked shade, and as the door magically sealed, Gizem engaged it in combat while Rufus provided cover fire. Sidra unleashed radiant magic, destroying the shade, but the encounter plunged the parlor deck into darkness.

As the singer fled, she warned Sidra and Ahmet — the two who had pursued her — to seek out the Ghost of March. In Osman’s quarters, he discovered his journal had been stolen, and his memory of its contents felt locked away. Meanwhile, Sidra and Ahmet found a freshly cleared ritual circle in the cargo hold, witnessing the singer and her accomplices — among them the hunched Mervyn — vanish after completing a teleportation spell. Realizing that a spell rebound had breached the hull, Sidra and Ahmet alerted the Greek Captain, who began hailing a nearby vessel for assistance as the ship began to take on water.
