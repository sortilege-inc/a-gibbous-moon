Night 8 · Session 8 · 2025-12-14

The Resting Place of Vlad Tepes

Summary

The party explored a chamber where they discovered silver coins and stones, along with an Undead Priest consuming the corpses of several other priests. As they processed this grim sight, they heard Turkish-speaking voices approaching. Sidra al-Najjar activated her innate sorcery, crushing a bead to release a cloud of dust that hindered the advancing men.

Two armed men confronted the group, one wielding a polearm and the other a handgun. They hesitated upon seeing Sidra and Gizem Faruki, demanding to know the party's purpose. Sidra claimed they were there to prevent undead harm and had taken nothing, prompting the men to defer to their leader, Hayriah, who soon arrived. Hayriah, a tomb-raider dressed in expedition gear, confronted Gizem and demanded the return of any taken items and an immediate withdrawal.

Sidra cast Sacred Flame on the Undead Priest, illuminating the room and alarming the armed men. Hayriah ordered her crew to escort the party into a larger room to clear the site, attempting to split the group. Jean-Zera Mongé used his bardic talents to unsettle the Undead Priest, while Miray, a druid, empowered her quarterstaff and attacked but only managed to knock aside a limb. Jean-Zera then struck decisively, disemboweling the Undead Priest and killing it.

An armed man, startled by Jean-Zera's bloodied appearance, fired a panicked shot, wounding him and ordering him to kneel. Hayriah advanced with crackling energy in her staff, zapping Jean-Zera's hand to force him to drop his knife and demanding Gizem's compliance. Refusing to submit, Gizem retaliated, striking two escorts and blocking their advance. Miray unleashed Moonbeam, blasting an armed man and making the group appear as a greater threat in Hayriah's eyes.

Sidra supported the effort by casting Fire Bolt at a gunman, igniting through the Moonbeam's mist and drawing Hayriah's focus. In a desperate attempt to touch Sidra, Hayriah stepped into the Moonbeam, suffering catastrophic damage from the radiant light. Jean-Zera capitalized on the moment, using Dissonant Whispers to force a panicked retreat from a gunman inside the Moonbeam, further injuring him. As the situation unfolded, Sidra's djinn surged with terror, and a cold mist coalesced, heralding the arrival of a Vampire that materialized from the mist, declaring it felt good to be free.

The Vampire conjured floating blood-dripping blades, killing Hayriah and another man outright. The party debated their options, weighing the risks of fleeing against the necessity of fighting as the Vampire blocked their exit. Miray repositioned Moonbeam directly onto the Vampire, whose scream confirmed its weakness to radiant damage. The combined efforts of the party ultimately led to the Vampire's destruction, its body evaporating into antiquated armor that clattered to the floor.

With the immediate threat neutralized, Radu the Handsome, a ghostly figure, appeared to thank the group for freeing him from his brother, the Vampire, expressing that his burden was lifted. The party inspected the Bound Casket, finding it empty but noting that its chains were broken and runic wards remained active. Sidra's djinn expressed relief that the existential threat had passed.

Continuing their exploration, they uncovered a private library and a shrine to John III Sobieski, collecting valuable artifacts, including a karabela saber and a family banner. Their search revealed a scorpion nest, prompting Sidra to use Thunderwave to clear the corridor of scorpions. Gizem engaged in combat with the swarms, while Miray was grappled by the broodmother. The party worked together to defeat the broodmother and harvest valuable materials for alchemical use.

In a cabinet room, they discovered correspondence between Mehmed II and Vlad, revealing historical insights. They also uncovered a laboratory with a viscous black liquid, identified later as a Potion of Necrotic Resistance. After a series of encounters with undead guardians, the party successfully defeated them, collecting their arms and armor.

Upon exiting the ruins after extensive underground exploration, they stashed their major loot in a Bathhouse Safehouse. Ahra 'the Wolf' thanked them for resolving the site and informed them that Osman wished to speak with them regarding a journal returned to him, which was revealed to be a fake. Osman reported that the Ghost of March demanded a rendezvous in the Undercity to deliver the true journal. The party agreed to arrange the meeting while considering Osman's safety.
