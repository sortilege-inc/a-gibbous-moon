Night 4 · Session 4 · 2025-10-26

The Albasti, and Entry to the Undercity

Summary

In the bustling streets of Constantinople, Sidra al-Najjar wandered away from the mosque area and found herself in a quiet corridor lined with warehouses and residences, feeling a sense of unease. Ahmet Yusuf, a young urchin, stealthily followed a suspicious pair discussing temporary housing, while Jean-Zera Mongé, a half-elf bard, shadowed them from behind to remain undetected. Sidra, curious, approached from a parallel lane, drawn into the unfolding mystery.

An uncanny winged creature resembling a woman materialized before them, taking the hand of the lurer and approaching the unsuspecting mark in the alley. A wave of supernatural dread washed over the group, halting their advance. Jean-Zera whispered encouragement to Ahmet, urging him to stay focused. At the mosque kitchen, Gizem Faruki engaged in a theological discussion with Nadja, a volunteer, about the inclusion of magically arisen beings in Islamic practice, sharing her Sufi perspective.

Ahmet intervened, pretending to know the mark and pulling him away from the creature's grasp. The albasti, a dream-feeding predator, recoiled as Ahmet steadied the man. Jean-Zera recognized the creature's method of inducing sleep and recalled its lore, while Sidra, shaken, sought refuge on a rooftop to observe the scene below. Her djinn warned her of the leech-like entity preying on the unsuspecting.

The albasti's face transformed into a grotesque vent-like grille as it placed a hand over Ahmet's eyes, magically forcing him into a deep slumber. In response, Jean-Zera unleashed dissonant whispers, inflicting psychic pain on the devotee and compelling him to flee. As Ahmet succumbed to the creature's dream, he found himself in a blazing desert mirage, where a veiled figure offered help before dissolving into sand. Sidra, determined to fight back, crushed a Nazar bead and cursed the albasti, hoping to impair its next attack.

Her attempt to cast Fire Bolt backfired, linking through the albasti to a victim elsewhere, causing a piercing scream to echo back. The mark later returned to the mosque, flustered and reporting a near-mugging initiated by a child, prompting Gizem to rush to the alleyway trouble. Upon arrival, she confronted the albasti, but her first strike missed as the creature lunged at her with clawing hands.

In the dream, Ahmet realized the landscape was a contrived illusion and chose to break free. The painful awakening inflicted a brutal psychic rebound, causing him to collapse to the ground. Gizem quickly stabilized him and invoked Shield of Faith, raising a shimmering ward as she turned back to the albasti. Jean-Zera's Healing Word brought Ahmet back to consciousness, allowing him to rejoin the fray. With newfound determination, Ahmet struck the albasti with his quarterstaff, which twisted into thorned wood, delivering a heavy blow.

Sidra called down Sacred Flame from the rooftop, scorching the creature, while Gizem unleashed a flurry of blows, ultimately battering the albasti unconscious. The group assessed the inert body, binding it and wrapping it in a discarded rug before carrying it away from the dangerous part of town. At Hano's, a sharp-eyed monster-hunting patron, they presented the albasti's body, earning strong approval and a reward of 25 gold, along with a magical handaxe for Gizem.

As they discussed their next steps, Hano praised Ahra 'the Wolf', the Janissary leader, for her recent recruits. Sidra's djinn warned that Jean-Zera's intense cursing had thinned the veil to the land of the dead, risking nearby corpse animation, but thankfully, none occurred. Later, Jean-Zera researched the cursed dream in Osman Hamdi Bey's library, uncovering cases of long-term cursed sleep but no identified predator source. Ahmet studied a rare tome, revealing a hidden history of magical duels and non-human interventions.

That evening, Ahmet sought intel at a bathhouse, learning about the Thieves Bazaar and the Ghost of March, while the group shared a vivid dream of a little girl cradling a dead cat that twitched back to life. In the morning, Sidra visited her mother, who provided her with a toll token for the Undercity, urging caution. Gizem and Sidra then visited Nazif’s Scents in the Grand Bazaar, where they arranged passage to Kore and descended into the bustling Undercity.

Inside the Arraf Tavern, they met Kore, a beautiful singer, who discussed Sufism and inclusion for non-humans with Gizem. Kore gifted Gizem an Undercity coin pass, ensuring they would not be dependent on intermediaries. Meanwhile, Jean-Zera met Chique Manu, who hinted at his father's debts and invited him to the Karakoy Casino that night. As the group reconvened, they agreed to explore the Undercity further, investigate the graveyard disturbances, and accept Chique Manu's casino invitation, all while keeping their shared dream details close.

Moments

  • Uncanny Encounter in the Alley: In a dimly lit alley, a winged being resembling a woman with spindly limbs emerged, capturing the attention of those nearby. The creature approached the lurer, taking their hand with unsettling grace, before turning towards the unsuspecting mark. Jean-Zera felt an instinct urging him to stay back, sensing that the unfolding scene would not end well. He whispered to Ahmet, warning him of the danger ahead, yet both remained frozen, uncertain of their next move. The atmosphere thickened with tension as the figure continued its advance, manifesting a predator among the streets.
  • Jean-Zera's Dissonant Whispers: As Jean-Zera Mongé cast dissonant whispers at the human helper, the air filled with a haunting melody. The whispers inflicted psychic pain, causing the helper to stagger and drop Ahmet Yusuf, who tumbled to the ground. The helper turned and fled down the street. Ahmet was left behind as the chaos settled around them. Jean-Zera observed the effects of his spell.
  • Ahmet's Despair in the Dreamscape: Ahmet awakened in a blazing desert, with dunes stretching endlessly in all directions under the relentless sun. He turned to see a veiled figure standing before him, who stated, "You are not equipped for this landscape. Let me help you." As the figure offered assistance, it dissolved into sand, and the mirage of an oasis evaporated, leaving Ahmet in the oppressive heat.
  • Ahmet's Thorned Quarterstaff Strike: As Ahmet Yusuf spoke an incantation in an unknown language, his quarterstaff transformed into a twisted, thorned weapon. He swung the staff at the albasti, delivering a heavy blow that significantly contributed to the creature's defeat.
  • Chique Manu's Casino Invitation: After a large breakfast, Chique Manu greeted Jean-Zera, saying, "It's good to see you! How's your father?" Jean-Zera replied that he hadn't heard from his father in a while and mentioned a chance encounter in Southeast France. Chique Manu hinted at having taken Jean-Zera's father's return fare, implying a connection to gambling. He then suggested, "Have you been to the Karakoy? You should come tonight as my escort, though you're responsible for your own pocketbook." Jean-Zera hesitated, considering the implications of the invitation and the underlying motives behind it.
  • Nazif's Passage to the Undercity: Nazif the perfumer, a tall figure in a loose shirt, guided Gizem and Sidra through a rear curtain after accepting Sidra’s toll coin. He opened an old stone door leading down a set of worn stairs into a spacious stone hall, adorned with carpets and illuminated by flickering lights. As they stepped into the bustling Undercity, they were greeted by a vibrant marketplace filled with altered folk, including half-orcs, half-elves, and tieflings. The guards at the exit offered directions to the Arraf Tavern.
  • Invitation to the Crowning Parade: Osman Hamdi Bay approached Jean-Zera Mongé and expressed his delight at their encounter. He revealed that there would be a crowning ceremony parade in a few days and extended an invitation for Jean-Zera and his companions to attend this significant event in the city. Jean-Zera accepted the invitation on behalf of the group, acknowledging the importance of the occasion. The conversation hinted at the possibility of further adventures, including a potential excursion to a local cemetery, as they prepared for the upcoming festivities.
