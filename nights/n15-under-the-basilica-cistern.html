Under the Basilica Cistern

Summary

The party descended into the flooded Basilica Cistern beneath Constantinople, where the air was thick with moisture and the sound of dripping water echoed ominously. As they approached the entrance, two Mansur Enforcers stood guard, but Jean-Zera skillfully convinced them to allow passage under the pretense of official business. The guards warned the group of dangers lurking deeper within, mentioning only the tiny Bichura spirits they had encountered.

With torchlight in hand, the adventurers ventured along the raised wooden walkways, where Sidra’s magic softened their movements. Their exploration led them to a burlap bag hanging precariously from a platform, which Jean-Zera retrieved using his Mage Hand. Inside, they discovered a pristine hand drum, preserved by the bag’s magical properties. Meanwhile, Gizem’s keen eyes spotted a submerged mirror, revealing an unfamiliar soldier with East Asian features. When she waved, the soldier reacted cautiously, but communication proved impossible through the glass.

As the party continued, Sidra was targeted by a mischievous spirit attempting to pick her pocket. In a gesture of goodwill, she offered baklava, which the spirit accepted, disappearing into the depths. The atmosphere shifted when a section of the boardwalk collapsed, dropping Sidra and Miray into spiked water, revealing a hunter’s trap. Two Merrows emerged from the depths to attack, but Gizem managed to pull Sidra to safety while the others engaged in combat. The skirmish was fierce, with spells and arrows flying as the party fought back against the Merrows, ultimately overcoming them through a combination of strategy and magic.

Further exploration yielded skeletons, ancient coins, and a sealed waterproof metal box containing potions and a diary belonging to the scholar Petrus Gyllius. The diary revealed dark secrets about the cistern’s history, including the disposal of magic users’ bodies and a mention of a dreaded she-devil. As they pressed on, the Black Queen Chess Piece they carried vibrated near a large Medusa statue, triggering a mechanism that drained the water and revealed a staircase. The Medusa, now animated, declared that none would free the snake demoness, prompting a hasty retreat from the party.

Miray transformed into a crocodile and swam back toward the mirror, while the Medusa unleashed arrows, wounding Jean-Zera. The party retaliated with ranged attacks, but the Medusa’s petrifying gaze turned one Merrow to stone. In a chaotic exchange, Gizem landed a stunning strike on the Medusa, allowing the group to escape down the newly revealed stairs into an ancient prison complex.

Once inside, the party took a moment to rest and recover, during which Jean-Zera attuned to the hand drum. Their investigation revealed cursed keys and a fire elemental trapped in one cell, which they narrowly avoided. In another cell, they discovered the remains of a couple from Justinian’s era, along with a spellbook containing useful spells. As they explored further, they encountered a richly furnished cell housing Mahidevran Hatun, Suleiman’s favorite concubine, who claimed to have been secluded for only a few days.

The party also met a red-dressed prisoner who called herself the Shahmeran and claimed unjust imprisonment during a court purge. After deliberating the risks, the group decided to free her, prying apart the damaged cell door. As the anti-magic field dissipated, she erupted into manic laughter, revealing herself to be a powerful sorceress who had merely played along with their assumption that she was the Shahmeran.
