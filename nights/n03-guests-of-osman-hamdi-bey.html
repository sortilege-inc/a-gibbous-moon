Night 3 · Session 3 · 2025-10-19

Guests of Osman Hamdi Bey

Summary

The party found themselves aboard the sinking ship, the Ejder-i Derya. A djinn had been banished, and a woman used as an exorcism decoy had escaped via teleportation with accomplices. They learned that Osman Hamdi Bay, their prominent patron, had his journal stolen, and his memories regarding it obscured. Below decks, the crew worked diligently to bail water and rig a pump, while the breached hull appeared to have been magically remade. The ship’s captain announced that the emergency had stabilized, and they would head directly to Constantinople.

As the ship continued its journey, another vessel rendezvoused with them, prompting the captain to order passengers to remain in their rooms unless escorted. Among the new arrivals was Crown Prince Abd-ul Mejid, who greeted Osman Hamdi Bay with muted formality. During their conversation, the party learned of the Caliph's death and that Osman would assist the Crown Prince during the transition. Osman invited the party to stay at his house in Constantinople for the night before receiving a task in the morning, clarifying that they were free guests and not under house arrest.

In the following hours, Sidra al-Najjar expressed her desire to send a letter to her mother in the city, which Osman permitted for delivery the next day. Meanwhile, Ahmet Yusuf handed Jean-Zera Mongé a letter to deliver to his mother via a bathhouse in the Kasimpasha District. Upon arriving at Osman’s mansion, the group was shown multiple libraries, artifact rooms, and a row of furnished guest rooms with staff assistance. Jean-Zera explored the library, discovering a locked glass-front case filled with magical tomes in Greek and ancient Near Eastern languages, while Ahmet browsed the collection, recognizing many languages but also encountering unfamiliar scripts.

As the evening progressed, Sidra inwardly addressed the presence within her, seeking clarity about its intentions. The internal presence responded, claiming it could influence few things in the world and sought to aid her actions. Ahmet discovered a study containing a grand piano and a detailed map of Constantinople, marked with locations of notable underworld figures and sites. The party also encountered Ashraf, a curmudgeonly talking tortoise caretaker, who managed the home in Osman’s absence.

Gizem Faruki reviewed texts on memory alteration, noting various methods of erasing or implanting memories. The group recalled that Osman had written in a personal cipher, and without the journal, even he could not access its contents. Jean-Zera cast Detect Magic in the library, overwhelmed by the strong auras emanating from the books and curios. He selected Aramaic and Latin tomes of interest to request permission to copy later.

That night, each member of the party shared a strange dream involving a forest path, a house that “wanted to be remembered,” and a child whose accidental damage to a grandfather clock was mysteriously reversed. The next morning, Osman explained that Ashraf’s speech arose from wild magic exposure and that he needed the stolen journal to clear his mind. He then deputized the party to recover the journal under the auspices of “A Certain Community,” the Empire’s clandestine service, offering resources and discretion.

Osman provided Jean-Zera with a sealed letter to deliver to Ahra "the Wolf," a Janissary leader, at the Banished Angels in the Kasimpasha District. Gizem checked her bakery, finding her assistant and the assistant’s younger brother managing operations. Jean-Zera used illusory script to compose a coded report to his father, arranging its relay via Rufus Dimitrious, a trusted intermediary. After checking in with their families, the party reconvened and followed directions into a seedy alley, where they encountered two opium-stupefied individuals who were, in fact, alert and surveilling their approach.

Inside the Janissary stronghold, the party met Ahra "the Wolf," who agreed to help find Osman’s journal in exchange for the party infiltrating criminal operations while the Janissaries rebuilt their strength. After requesting privacy to confer, they identified key underworld figures for infiltration, ultimately choosing to start with Hano "the Widow." Hano tasked them with investigating a bounty hunter who had fallen into a strange fever. Upon examining the fevered man, the party debated whether his condition was due to a contagious disease or a spiritual affliction. Sidra confirmed it was not the work of a djinn, while Jean-Zera detected a magical tether to an external entity feeding on the man's mind.

With the background of the case established, the party learned that the man was a tailor and prolific monster hunter who had recently searched for a quarry near a mosque soup kitchen. Ahmet, familiar with the area, guided the group toward the larger hub mosque known for aiding the poor. As they gathered information, they observed a suspicious man soliciting lodging from the crowd, prompting Jean-Zera to stealthily follow him, encouraged by Sidra, while the others maintained their positions at the mosque.
