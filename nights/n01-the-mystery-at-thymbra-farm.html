The Mystery at Thymbra Farm

Summary

At Thymbra Farm, the Calvert family farmhouse north of Constantinople, the party gathered for an evening meal with notable figures, including Osman Hamdi Bey, General Patrice de MacMahon, and Heinrich and Sophia Schliemann, who were connected to a nearby archaeological dig. Frederick Calvert discussed the ongoing excavation of an ancient Greek site, highlighting promising finds despite a reduced crew following a recent festival.

The dinner unfolded primarily in French, with occasional interjections in Turkish and English. Osman Hamdi Bey introduced Gizem Faruki, Sidra al-Najjar, and Ahmet Yusuf, praising the younger attendees for their assistance at the dig site. Sidra, a veiled 13-year-old girl, and Ahmet, a ten-year-old errand-runner, sat at the end of the main table, attempting to mirror the comportment of their adult counterparts. Gizem, dressed in white, gold, and blue attire, offered small treats to the children and prevented Ahmet from indulging in an alcoholic drink.

Jean-Zera Mongé, the half-elf bard and antiquarian, charmed the guests with his engaging demeanor, sketching attendees and playing a fashionable piano piece. Rufus Dimitrious, the Greek sailor-rogue, observed quietly from the periphery of the fire, accepting the Calverts' hospitality while keeping a watchful eye on the wealthy guests. Evelyn Calvert guided discussions on weighty topics such as war and trade, while Sophia contributed more tentatively.

In a private moment, Frank Calvert introduced Rufus as a trusted coastal guide for excursions, masking his discreet dealings in logistics. Frank paid Rufus 50 gold for arranging smuggling logistics and identifying suitable coastal coves. Frank then summoned Heinrich, prompting Rufus to discreetly fetch him. While attempting to eavesdrop, Rufus caught snippets of mention regarding “the hill” and “the key” before a creaking floorboard alerted them, leading to the door being shut.

As the evening progressed, Jean-Zera probed Osman about the dig, learning that the team might have located a megaron, a civic building in good condition, and was working to access additional rooms. Rufus entertained Sidra and Ahmet with tall tales of hunting mermaids and riding leviathans, but the children reacted skeptically. Dessert brought praise for Gizem's baklava, enjoyed alongside Turkish coffee.

Before dawn, Gizem practiced Sufi turning on the terrace, drawing the interest of Sophia, who inquired about the dervish practice. Gizem demonstrated basic turning steps and their devotional significance, inspiring nearby younger girls to imitate the movements. Sidra experienced an inner conflict, recalling that some referred to dervishes as “djinn-possessed,” stirring a curiosity linked to her own unsettling djinn-touched magic.

Ahmet explored the house and grounds, slipping into the Schliemanns' unoccupied guest quarters, noting the luggage and furnishings. Later that morning, Rufus and General de MacMahon, both nursing hangovers, shared breakfast drinks on the veranda. Suddenly, a loud crack echoed across Thymbra Farm, and a flowering tree in front of the veranda instantly desiccated, leaching color from the surrounding ground and leaving the onlookers' ears ringing.

Moments

  • Dinner at Thymbra Farm: At Thymbra Farm's Calvert farmhouse, Gizem Faruki, Sidra al-Najjar, Jean-Zera Mongé, Rufus Dimitrious, and Ahmet Yusuf gathered for dinner with notable guests Osman Hamdi Bey, General Patrice de MacMahon, and the Calvert brothers, Frederick and Frank. Discussions centered on the nearby archaeological dig of an ancient Greek city and the recent reductions to the excavation crew following a local festival. The children, including Ahmet, sat apart from the adults, listening to the conversations that filled the English-style decorated interior of the farmhouse.
  • Osman Hamdi Bey's Public Praise: Osman Hamdi Bey stood before the assembled guests and introduced Gizem Faruki, Sidra al-Najjar, and Ahmet Yusuf. He expressed gratitude for their assistance at the dig site, noting their courteousness and responsiveness to the demands of the excavation. "Thank you for your patience with me on this," he said, highlighting their contributions despite their short time at the site. He acknowledged Sidra and Ahmet's efforts in fetching water and supporting the crew, emphasizing the importance of their roles. The atmosphere buzzed with recognition as the guests noted the young helpers being honored publicly.
  • Frank's Covert Deal with Rufus: Frank Calvert publicly introduced Rufus Dimitrious as a trusted coastal guide, embellishing his reputation with tales of excursions along the coast and beautiful sights. In a private exchange, Frank discreetly paid Rufus fifty gold pieces for arranging smuggling logistics. Following this, Rufus was sent to fetch Heinrich at Frank's request. While doing so, he overheard a conversation mentioning 'the hill' and 'the key.' A creaking floorboard alerted them, prompting the swift closure of a nearby door.
  • Excavation Revelations at the Dig: While fielding questions from General Patrice de MacMahon, Jean-Zera Mongé engaged Osman Hamdi Bey in conversation about the ongoing excavation. Jean-Zera learned that the excavation team had potentially uncovered a megaron, a significant civic building from an ancient Greek city, and that they were currently working to access additional rooms within it. The findings indicated that the structure was in relatively good condition, although they were still in the early stages of exploration.
  • Sufi Turning on the Terrace: Before dawn on the terrace, Gizem Faruki practiced Sufi turning on a balance nail board, her movements graceful and focused as she sought to master the art. Sophia approached and asked, "What are you doing?" Gizem explained the dance of the dervishes of Constantinople and offered to teach Sophia the steps. Nearby, younger girls watched with curiosity, attempting to imitate Gizem's movements, their awkward attempts resulting in giggles as they stumbled and fell. The terrace became a lively scene of learning and playful imitation.
  • Desiccation of the Flowering Tree: Shortly after ten, as Rufus Dimitrious and a hungover General Patrice de MacMahon took breakfast on the veranda, a loud crack echoed across Thymbra Farm, startling everyone. In front of them a flowering tree instantly desiccated, its vibrant leaves vanishing and leaving behind a stark, colorless trunk. The ground around the tree leached of color, creating a ghostly patch in the otherwise lively landscape. The assembly stood in shock, their ears ringing from the sudden sound.
