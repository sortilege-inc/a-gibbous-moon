Night 13 · Session 13 · 2026-03-02

The Defense of Yedikule Fortress

Summary

The Sublime Porte uncovered a plot by Hamit the Quartz of Damascus to assassinate the Vizier-i Aezam during peace talks between the Ottoman Empire and Egypt. To thwart this scheme, the Sublime Porte planned to use the Vizier as bait, luring Hamit to Yedikule Fortress with a decoy. The real Vizier was smuggled into a high-security cell while a decoy was displayed to attract Hamit’s attention.

Preparations included fortifying the fortress with traps and defensive measures, deploying veteran fighters disguised as prisoners, and strategically placing sleep gas grenades. The true identity of the prisoner was withheld from the Mansur Enforcers to prevent any moles from discovering the plan.

Under the cover of night, Hamit and his crew approached Yedikule, finding the fortress initially quiet. Their entry through the main gate escalated into chaos as Hamza engaged in melee combat, supported by Ziya and others providing ranged fire. Several hired guards were killed, and a guard inadvertently triggered a dart trap, complicating the defense.

The situation intensified when a recruited mage prisoner, whose restraints had been loosened, unleashed a Cone of Cold on the attackers, inflicting severe injuries. The mage then turned invisible and fled, leaving the defenders to face the onslaught. As the attackers pressed deeper into the fortress, they encountered the hidden veteran fighters, leading to a fierce close-quarters battle ignited by a fireball that scorched the corridor.

Despite sustaining injuries, the attackers consumed potions to recover and pushed toward the Royal Cell area where the decoy Vizier was held. Unexpected reinforcements prompted discussions of retreat through the sewers. Ultimately, Hamit was captured just before the diplomatic meeting, but his memory of encountering Jean-Zera Mongé was erased using a memory scroll.

In the antechamber adjacent to the formal meeting hall, Jean-Zera, Miray Ruba Mata Biru, Gizem Faruki, Sidra al-Najjar, and Osman Hamdi Bey awaited the proceedings. The Padishah, Abd-ul Mejid, announced Hamit’s capture, but the atmosphere shifted dramatically when Kavalan, a mysterious figure, rose and swiftly slit Hamit’s throat. As Hamit’s eyes glowed pale blue, Kavalan cast a spell akin to Speak with Dead, questioning him in his final moments.

Kavalan’s actions, driven by suspicion of memory manipulation, implicated the Topkapi Palace in foul play, casting a shadow over the ongoing peace talks. The public execution left the assembled diplomats uneasy, and an unidentified palace official hastily exited the negotiations, drawing the attention of both the party and the Padishah. The delicate balance of power was now at risk, and the implications of this brutal act would reverberate through the halls of the palace and beyond.
