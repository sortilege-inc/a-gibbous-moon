Night 7 · Session 7 · 2025-12-08

Echoes of the Undead

Summary

In the aftermath of the Festival of Candles, chaos erupted as the Ghost of March and his companions, clad in Sekban soldiers' uniforms, executed Nazif, a perfumer, accusing him of betrayal. This shocking event unfolded beside Chique Manu, who sat in stunned silence as the Bostanji Imperial Guard declared a state of emergency and swiftly moved to disperse the crowd. Gizem Faruki confirmed Nazif's death while assessing the scene. When approached by Miray Ruba Mata Biru for an audience, the Ghost of March cryptically promised a meeting soon before vanishing into the city with his followers.

As the guards restored order, the party regrouped at a late-night café, sharing insights about the Ghost of March and the implications of the festival's chaos. Gizem noted the Sekban uniforms worn by the Ghost's entourage, prompting speculation about the connection between Janissary-linked agitators and the Ghost's followers. The group decided to investigate Nazif's perfumery stall in the Grand Bazaar that night to determine who would control access to the Undercity following his death.

Utilizing stealth, the party covertly entered Nazif's darkened perfumery, where they encountered Sharif the Strong Fur, a black cat in a red fez. Sharif announced his new role overseeing undercity passage, enforcing a strict no-magic rule due to concerns over volatility. He requested Sidra al-Najjar's assistance with gravely ill cats and hinted at larger issues at play, while also revealing that Nazif had been relieved of his duties by the Ghost of March.

After a night of rest, the party enjoyed breakfast at a local bakery before heading to meet Ahra 'the Wolf' in the Kasimpasha District. Ahra had already informed Osman Hamdi Bey of their arrival. The party briefed her on the Ghost of March's actions at the festival, including Nazif's murder and the involvement of Sekban soldiers. Ahra confirmed their assignment to deal with the "unquiet dead" beneath the ruins of Saint Benoit Church as final payment for their services, noting her own preoccupations with the Ghost's activities.

Ahra disclosed that several state armories had been broken into overnight, coinciding with the Ghost's actions. She advised the party to acquire a map at the Grand Bazaar, as the only known undercity route ran through Nazif's location. The group learned that Saint Benoit, the first Catholic church, had been abandoned after an earthquake about 120 years ago, with locals reporting disturbing noises and rising dead in the area.

Jean-Zera Mongé procured a high-quality map revealing less obvious entrances to Saint Benoit, including a graveyard entrance. He also consulted Giannis the Cut, who indicated that their party had been underbid by Hayriah but agreed to a 25% cut if they produced results quickly. The group decided to enter the church via the graveyard during daylight to get ahead of other treasure seekers.

In the cemetery, they discovered a trapdoor leading into a dark corridor, which did not match their map, suggesting they were in a later-added basement beneath the church. As they explored, they encountered a suit of armor with red-glowing eyes that warned them against taking anything. Deeper in, they faced animated cadavers that attacked, exuding a poisonous secretion. Jean-Zera recognized the heraldry of House Tepes on one of the undead, linking them to a notorious lineage.

Sidra attempted to communicate with the undead in Romanian, but understanding remained elusive. The party combined their efforts to dispatch the cadavers, though maggot-like parasites briefly entered a companion's mouth before being expelled. A magical presence deeper in the complex suggested some force sustained the undead.

In a larger chamber resembling an arena, the party encountered a noble-seeming male ghost who proposed a mission: to slay his brother Vlad, bound within the ruins, so he could finally leave. The ghost warned that shedding blood would empower Vlad, advising caution. The group chose to delay confronting Vlad and instead explored other areas, discovering a peaceful prayer room and a genuine torture chamber.

As they cleared debris, they uncovered a breached chamber where a ghoul attacked, throwing a severed limb at the party. At the same time, voices speaking Turkish signaled the approach of humanoids.

Moments

  • Ahra 'the Wolf''s Alarming Revelation of Weapon Thefts: Ahra 'the Wolf' disclosed that several state armories had been broken into overnight, leading to the theft of large caches of state weapons. She connected these incidents to the Sekban of Ghost activity, raising concerns about the implications of such a breach. The mention of Ghost of March executing perceived betrayals hinted at deeper conflicts and trust issues within the community. The conversation reflected a growing unease about the state of security and the potential for further chaos. As the group processed this information, it became clear that immediate action might be necessary to address the unfolding crisis.
  • Sharif's Rule Enforced with a Slash: As a spellcasting gesture began in Nazif’s stall, Sharif moved to enforce his rule against magic in the area. With a quick slash, he cut the caster's ankle, causing immediate pain and bleeding. "You can use magic anywhere else, but not here," he declared. The sudden act shocked those present, making it clear that Sharif would not tolerate any breach of his regulations. The caster winced, realizing the consequences of their actions in Sharif's domain.
  • Sharif's Request for Cat Care: Sharif approached Sidra al-Najjar, expressing gratitude for her past assistance in the Undercity and revealing that he had four gravely ill cats in need of help. He invited her to return the following day to coordinate assistance, emphasizing the urgency of the situation while acknowledging the busyness of the current night. Sidra, eager to help, vouched for her companions to ensure they could join her in this endeavor. Sharif agreed, noting his preference to keep magic out of the equation during their work to simplify matters and avoid potential volatility.
  • Jean-Zera Mongé Identifies House Tepes Heraldry: As the group delved deeper into the mysteries surrounding the abandoned church, Jean-Zera Mongé's keen eye caught a glimpse of heraldry on one of the undead that had risen from the shadows. "This is the sigil of House Tepes!" he exclaimed. The revelation linked the undead to a lineage steeped in dark history. With this newfound knowledge, the party braced themselves for the challenges that lay ahead.
  • Spectral Arena and the Noble Ghost: As the group entered a vast chamber resembling an arena, they observed spectral crowds of ghosts seated around the edges, their translucent forms flickering in the dim light. In the center, a handsome noble-looking ghost stood, addressing the adventurers with a warm greeting, "Adventurers here at last!" He revealed that he had been watching their exploits and expressed his weariness of the place, stating, "I haven't had visitors in so long." Ghost of March explained that he was bound to this location to ensure his brother, Vlad, did not escape and harm others, urging the group to slay him so he could finally leave.
  • Exploring the Torture Chamber and Ghoul Encounter: After a thorough examination of nearby rooms, Sidra al-Najjar, Jean-Zera Mongé, Gizem Faruki, and Ahmet Yusuf decided to postpone their confrontation with Vlad. They ventured into a serene Christian prayer room before discovering a grim torture chamber, which contained a modest storeroom with a small box holding 50 silver pieces. Their exploration took a darker turn when they uncovered a breached chamber, where a ghoul clad in tattered priestly vestments hurled a severed limb at them, preparing to attack as ominous Turkish voices echoed from behind.
