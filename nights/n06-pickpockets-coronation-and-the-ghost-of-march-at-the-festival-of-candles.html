Night 6 · Session 6 · 2025-12-01

Pickpockets, Coronation, and the Ghost of March at the Festival of Candles

Summary

As the sun dipped below the horizon, Sidra al-Najjar, Jean-Zera Mongé, Gizem Faruki, and Ahmet Yusuf navigated the labyrinthine alleys of the Undercity, aiming to reach Kore's theater for a performance. Ahmet, the resourceful scout, led the search for a buyer for a book they had acquired but ultimately decided against selling it.

Their journey took a perilous turn when Jean-Zera noticed a hand belonging to a gnome named Frank slipping into his pocket. The situation escalated as they became aware of a group of muggers—Elveda, Kasap Yusuf, Melek, Ejder, and others—positioning themselves to encircle the party. In a moment of desperation, Sidra attempted to charm Frank, but he resisted and demanded money instead.

Tensions mounted as Ejder brandished a weapon and threatened the group. Jean-Zera unleashed a wave of psychic energy, causing Ejder to stagger back in pain. Ahmet attempted to strike Elveda with his quarterstaff but missed as she kept her distance. Kasap Yusuf charged at Gizem, swinging his axe wildly but failing to connect.

Melek cast an unseen spell, while Ejder struck Jean-Zera with a well-aimed stone. Gizem seized the opportunity to deliver a series of strikes against the orc, injuring him. However, Frank drew a pistol and shot Jean-Zera, grazing him before disappearing behind crates.

The dwarf leader, Usta Kerim, emerged from the shadows, throwing a dagger that struck Sidra. In retaliation, she unleashed a magical rebuke, burning the dwarf despite his attempts to evade. Ahmet advanced and incapacitated Elveda with a well-placed blow, while the orc continued his reckless assault, missing his targets.

Jean-Zera attempted to reposition but was struck down by the orc's heavy axe. Usta Kerim then engaged Sidra, wounding her with his scimitar. Ahmet transformed into a black fox, joining the fray. Despite his efforts, the orc managed to down Jean-Zera once more, leaving the bard vulnerable.

Sidra cast Sleep on the orc, but he shook off the spell, while Melek stabilized Jean-Zera. Ejder picked up the cowering Elveda and retreated, shouting in thieves' cant. Frank reappeared, taking aim at the black fox, but the creature evaded his attack.

The orc grabbed Sidra and threw her aside, inflicting damage. In retaliation, Sidra blasted Usta Kerim with fire, singeing his beard. Ahmet leapt from above to bite the dwarf, disrupting his stance. Gizem delivered a series of nonlethal kicks, knocking Usta Kerim unconscious.

With the immediate threat neutralized, the orc paused, demanding payment to let the party go. A talking street cat named Grey, perched on Sidra's shoulder, invited her to seek the Council of the Ninth Life, offering to speak to Sharif on her behalf. The young muggers, under Usta Kerim's control, deliberated their options, expressing a desire for a safer place to go.

The group discovered a heavy coin purse on Usta Kerim, containing 58 gold pieces. Gizem took 10 gold for herself and arranged for the rest to be given to the children. When Frank attempted to keep the money, Jean-Zera disarmed him and reclaimed the bag for the youths. At a nearby dining-theater space, Kore agreed to provide safe lodging for the children, with the group covering the cost.

Kore briefed the party on the governance of the Undercity, mentioning the Ghost of March, a leader who maintained order among the neighborhoods. Upon request, Kore promised to put in a good word for the group to meet this enigmatic figure. Ahmet transformed back into his human form, disclosing his dual identity as a halfling druid who cared for children and warded against the evil eye.

That night, the four companions experienced a shared dream of a father figure being struck down by a mother wielding a bloodied desk clock, with Jean-Zera identifying a powerful enchantment influencing the mother. The following morning, Jean-Zera rode with Osman Hamdi Bey to the parade route near Topkapi Palace, where the others converged. Osman remarked on the significance of the royal family being honored by such a parade.

As the parade unfolded, the Bostanji Imperial Guard marched with grandeur, and Abd-ul Mejid received the Sword of Osman at the steps of the Hagia Sophia. Tensions flared when attendees threw coins back into the crowd, shouting that the gold belonged to the Janissaries. Osman urged the party to withdraw from the public scene, leading them to a private brunch room.

During brunch, Osman asserted that the Janissaries had been entirely destroyed and replaced by modern forces, treating Jean-Zera's surprise as naïveté. Ahmet circulated bathhouses for gossip, learning that those who had shouted at the parade faced severe repercussions. He inspected a ransacked house, determining that the search was targeted rather than random.

Meanwhile, Sidra explored a private library for alchemical recipes, discovering plausible formulas with obscure ingredient names. Papa Mert allowed her to observe his work, enhancing her knowledge. Gizem attended a community strategy meeting, where progressive co-religionists urged her to leverage her access to Osman to promote pluralism and protect minority communities.

That evening, the group joined Chique Manu at an outdoor table near a shadow theater, where animated shadows stole his ring mid-performance. Jean-Zera covertly cast Mage Hand to chase the shadows, while Gizem parkoured to the roof, retrieving the ring. Grateful, Chique Manu rewarded the group with a banknote worth approximately 100 gold.

As the night progressed, Jean-Zera noted Chique Manu's habitual touching of the ring, confirming its significance. The atmosphere shifted when tortoises carrying candles were spotted, and figures on adjacent rooftops observed the area. Suddenly, flash-powder explosions erupted, followed by distant gunshots. The Ghost of March appeared, executing the perfumer Mosolite Nazif for his perceived betrayals.
