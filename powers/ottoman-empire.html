Ottoman Empire

The Ottoman Empire is a significant political entity known for its vast territory and cultural influence. Osman Hamdi Bey serves as a royal polymath within the Empire, overseeing scholarly pursuits and the management of artifacts. The Empire enforces strict regulations on the exportation of artifacts, asserting that any items discovered within its borders must remain in Ottoman territory. This principle is underscored in discussions regarding discoveries related to ancient Troy, where Osman Hamdi Bey emphasizes the serious consequences of looting.

In a recent investigation near Constantinople, Osman Hamdi Bey interacted with Heinrich Schliemann, Frank Calvert, Jean-Zera Mongé, Rufus Dimitrious, Sidra al-Najjar, and Ahmet Yusuf while exploring a site connected to ancient Troy. The group uncovered artifacts and engaged with supernatural elements. Following these discoveries, Osman Hamdi Bey received an urgent message that required his immediate return to Constantinople, leading him to recruit Jean-Zera and his companions for an undisclosed task.

Osman Hamdi Bey's authority is evident as he negotiates the purchase of various antiquities, ensuring they remain within the Empire. This includes transactions for items retrieved from a Serpent's nest, which he acquires for a total of 80 silver pieces. Osman Hamdi Bey is portrayed as a knowledgeable and strategic figure, managing the complexities of cultural heritage while preserving the integrity of the Empire's historical assets.
