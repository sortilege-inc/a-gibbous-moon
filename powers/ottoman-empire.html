Ottoman Empire

The Ottoman Empire is a great political power, renowned for the vastness of its territory and the breadth of its cultural sway. Osman Hamdi Bey serves within it as a royal polymath, overseeing scholarly pursuits and the keeping of antiquities. The Empire enforces a strict rule upon the export of such artifacts, holding that whatsoever is unearthed within its borders must remain upon Ottoman ground — a principle underscored in the discussions touching the discoveries at ancient Troy, where Osman Hamdi Bey pressed home the grave consequences of looting.

In a recent inquiry near Constantinople, Osman Hamdi Bey fell into company with Heinrich Schliemann, Frank Calvert, Jean-Zera Mongé, Rufus Dimitrious, Sidra al-Najjar, and Ahmet Yusuf whilst exploring a site tied to ancient Troy. The party brought artifacts to light and had their dealings with matters supernatural. Following these discoveries, Osman Hamdi Bey received an urgent message that called for his immediate return to Constantinople, and so drew Jean-Zera and his companions into an undisclosed errand.

The authority of Osman Hamdi Bey shows plainly in his negotiating the purchase of sundry antiquities, that they might stay within the Empire — among them the items retrieved from a Serpent's nest, which he acquired for a total of eighty silver pieces. He is drawn throughout as a man of knowledge and strategy, managing the tangled business of cultural heritage while preserving the integrity of the Empire's historical treasures.
