Janissaries

The Janissaries, a clandestine faction operating in the shadows of Constantinople, are led by the formidable Ahra “the Wolf.” Once an elite military corps closely tied to the Ottoman Empire, the Janissaries now navigate a landscape of uncertainty following the death of the Caliph. Their primary mission is to restore their former strength and influence during this tumultuous power transition, positioning themselves as a vital force against threats to the populace, such as thieves and cutthroats.

Historically, the Janissaries were recognized for their disciplined structure and significant role in the empire's military, holding an elevated status within Turkish society akin to an elevated caste. However, they were officially disbanded over a generation ago during a conflict with the Second Bands, leading to their destruction and the obliteration of their headquarters. The remnants of their legacy have since been succeeded by the Imperial Guard, which serves the current regime. Despite their official dissolution, the Janissaries continue to resonate culturally among the populace. During a recent public event, attendees voiced loyalty with shouts of "This gold belongs to the Janissaries," particularly during the coronation of Abd-ul Mejid, which heightened tensions as citizens expressed their allegiance to the Janissaries, prompting a reaction from the security detail of Osman Hamdi Bey, who insisted that the Janissaries no longer exist.

Under Ahra's leadership, the Janissaries have established a reputation for their resourcefulness and discretion. Recently, they were tasked with recovering a stolen journal belonging to Osman Hamdi Bey, a mission that involved infiltrating key figures of the underworld, including Giannis the Cut “the Cut,” Hano “the Widow,” and Chique Manu. This operation is part of a broader strategy to eliminate dangers to the Ottoman Empire, emphasizing the urgency of their collaboration with external allies.

Chique Manu, a wealthy fixer and poet, aims to finance the Janissaries to reduce his house's dependency on external support and establish a more autonomous power structure. His ties to the Janissaries are highlighted through discussions about financing with Gizem Faruki and Jean-Zera Mongé at a clandestine gambling club, a meeting point for various factions and individuals. This setting reflects the competitive nature of the environment in which the Janissaries operate, as they navigate complex socio-political landscapes to strengthen their position without becoming overly reliant on any single ally.

The faction's influence extends into the Undercity, where the boundaries between the magical and mundane blur. Here, they have garnered respect and recognition, particularly from patrons like Hano 'the Widow,' who praised Ahra for her recent recruits. The Janissaries' members are not only skilled in combat but also adept at navigating complex social dynamics, as demonstrated during a recent confrontation with an albasti, a dream-feeding creature. In this encounter, members such as Miray, Jean-Zera Mongé, and Sidra al-Najjar showcased their abilities, combining physical prowess and magic to subdue the creature, which they later delivered to Hano 'the Widow' for compensation.

Despite their successes, the Janissaries face ongoing challenges, including remarks from Chique Manu that highlight their dependence on external resources, revealing struggles for autonomy within the faction. An abduction attempt on Chique Manu is thwarted by Gizem Faruki and Jean-Zera Mongé, illustrating the high-stakes environment surrounding the Janissaries and their allies. This incident, along with their successful intervention regarding Scavengers and mischievous fae creatures influenced by a witch disrupting their home, demonstrates the Janissaries' ongoing efforts to assert their influence while managing various threats and challenges.

Their members are actively engaged in seeking information and opportunities to bolster their standing, as seen when Ahmet sought guidance from Hano 'the Widow' on navigating the complexities of the Undercity. The Janissaries' intricate web of alliances and rivalries reflects their ongoing quest for power and stability in a world rife with danger and intrigue, as they strive to reclaim their place in a society that both honors and fears their legacy.

Most recently, the Janissaries have expanded their focus to support non-human and magical beings, standing as a counterforce to a certain community's efforts to suppress pluralism and prioritize human interests. They share a partnership with the enigmatic Ghost of March, who controls a series of interconnected buildings within the Thieves Bazaar, with both factions united in their opposition to the community's agenda. The Janissaries are characterized by a modern military approach reminiscent of the historical Sekban, with Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, a reputedly deceased leader of the Sekban, now commanding them. Alemdar has requested assistance in forming an alliance with Ahra, despite previous enmity, and has indicated that maintaining the appearance of ongoing conflict between the Janissaries and the Sekban would benefit their strategic interests.

In a recent development, the Janissaries have become embroiled in the underworld and political intrigue, engaging in covert operations that further solidify their influence. A member named Alexander defected and went missing after venturing into the catacombs, prompting Sidra al-Najjar and Jean-Zera Mongé to seek information about his whereabouts from Janissary contacts. Their efforts led them to the Padisha and the Sultan, where they were recognized as assets of Osman Hamdi Bey. The Sultan tasked them with identifying an assassin sent by Kavala Pasha, providing a scroll designed to erase any memory of their meeting from the assassin's mind. This incident underscores the Janissaries' adeptness at navigating political machinations and covert missions, further entrenching their role in the shifting power dynamics of Constantinople. Additionally, the discovery of a fresh corpse of a young Janissary in the catacombs indicates ongoing conflict, and Alexander's warnings of dangers within the catacombs, as well as his desire for revenge against those responsible for his wife's death, highlight the perilous environment in which the Janissaries operate.

The Janissaries are also associated with the military and political structure of the Ottoman Empire, linked to the enforcement of the Sultan's authority. They are known for their historical role as elite infantry units, with influence in secretive operations and policing. The faction maintains a presence through shadowy, semi-corporeal soldiers encountered during investigations related to a recent assassination attempt. These soldiers are armed and hostile, demonstrating the faction's potential threat. A commanding officer among them wielded a blade described by the Ghost of March, resembling a sword seen at the Janissary headquarters.
