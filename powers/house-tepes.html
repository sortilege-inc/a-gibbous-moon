House Tepes

House Tepes is a lineage notable for its historical ties to the undead and vampiric lore. The faction is linked to a noble ghost bound to the ruins of Saint Benoit Church for centuries. This ghost claims to be the brother of Vlad the Impaler and has commissioned the construction of chambers to contain Vlad and mitigate his murderous tendencies. House Tepes is further associated with the undead, as indicated by the heraldry found on animated cadavers encountered in the ruins.

Recently, during the Ghost of March festival, this ghost executed Nazif, a perfumist, accusing him of betrayal and asserting that his responsibilities had been supplanted by the Council of the Ninth Life. Following this act, Ghost of March's entourage, wearing uniforms of the disbanded Sekban soldiers, incited a state of emergency in the city, leading to chaos and speculation about connections between the Ghost of March and the former Janissaries, hinting at a possible underground resistance movement.

In the wake of Nazif's death, the gatekeeper Sharif the Strong Fur revealed that Ghost of March had relieved Nazif of his duties regarding undercity passage. Subsequently, a group was tasked with confronting the unquiet dead beneath Saint Benoit Church, where they encountered spectral crowds and Ghost of March of a nobleman who sought assistance with his brother, Vlad. Ghost of March warned that shedding blood would empower Vlad, indicating the perilous nature of the task ahead. As the group explored the ruins, they faced various undead threats and began to uncover the dark history tied to House Tepes and its ongoing influence within the city.
