A Certain Community

A Certain Community is the clandestine service of the Empire — a secret constabulary and intelligence bureau in one, a web of spies and agents that goes about its work well outside the formal apparatus of the law, and that instils a quiet unease in the few who know it exists. At its helm stands Osman Hamdi Bey, a patron of no small note, invested with the authority to deputise whom he pleases and to furnish such men with resources and a free hand for their errands. It is, in plainer terms, a state security apparatus, led by a figure of high education and many tongues who holds the interests of the Empire's humankind above all and sets his face against pluralism. Its creed is a single, harmonious voice in the councils of governance; its especial dread is the sway of the magical orders, against whom it will not scruple to turn magic itself. It reckons the present hour a chaos precisely for its multitude of voices, and — here the record turns upon itself — is given to faulting Osman for the very ecumenical breadth that the same passage names him its master.

Of late Osman Hamdi Bey has set the agents of A Certain Community to the recovery of his stolen journal, a matter grown urgent with the recent death of The Caliph, whose passing has opened a vacancy of power that draws the Community into the shifting business of the state. The agents are bidden to work their way into the dealings of sundry figures of the underworld — Hano "the Widow," Giannis the Cut, and Chique Manu amongst them — the better to gather intelligence and restore some order. The Community, for its part, keeps its own watch upon Osman and his affairs against the suspicion of corruption, and has taken pains to bind the name of Jean-Zera Mongé publicly to that of Giannis, the sooner to blacken his repute and hobble whatever dealings he might yet essay.

In their first meeting with Ahra "the Wolf," a captain of the Janissaries, the agents learn that their cooperation with A Certain Community will run beyond the mere recovery of the journal to a work of criminal infiltration meant to see the Janissaries regain their strength — the agents' purposes thus falling into step with the Community's own broader design of keeping the Empire in some semblance of stability.

Beyond its covert labours, the Certain Community keeps a foothold in the Undercity as well, where it turns its hand to the governance of that quarter and the welfare of its people. This aspect of the order answers to a figure styled the "Ghost of March," who is held to command a real legitimacy amongst the populace, and who bends his efforts to keeping order, curbing crime, and shielding the vulnerable — not least the children who fall under the sway of the local gangs. Not long since, members of the Certain Community, among them youths held in thrall by a dwarf named Mr. Karen, came to blows with a company of adventurers; these last had stepped in to guard the children from harm, and in the doing exposed the workings of power and protection in the Undercity. Having subdued Mr. Karen, the adventurers saw the children into safe hands through a contact named Kore, who undertook to lodge and protect them. That the Ghost of March should hold the reins of order here suggests that, for all its independence, the Certain Community remains answerable to his authority and his organisation — a further thread in the intricate web that A Certain Community weaves throughout the Empire.
