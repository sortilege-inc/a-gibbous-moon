Sekbans

The Sekbans are a faction entwined with the enigmatic figure known as the Ghost of March, drawing their identity from a disbanded military unit historically noted for dismantling the Janissaries. Members of the Sekbans are characterized by their striking uniforms, reminiscent of those worn by the Ghost's entourage during the celebrated Festival of Candles. Their notoriety surged following the assassination of Nazif, a perfumist accused of betrayal concerning the Council of the Nine Lives. This event prompted the Bostanji Imperial Guard to declare a state of emergency, forcing crowds to disperse while the Ghost of March and his followers withdrew into the city as additional guards responded to the chaos.

In the wake of Nazif's death, it became apparent that Sharif the Strong Fur, a black cat overseeing Nazif's former duties, had instituted a ban on magic within his realm, likely stemming from fears of volatility and secrecy accompanying magical practices. Investigations into Nazif’s connections to the Ghost revealed that the latter had previously relieved him of his position and was implicated in various incidents linked to the Sekbans. Notably, reports surfaced of state armories being breached in tandem with the Ghost's activities, hinting at a broader uprising or conspiracy that could unify the Sekbans and other underground forces into a cohesive resistance movement.

Not merely veterans, the Sekbans have become a symbolic alignment with the tradition of their forerunners, participating in organized underground operations that include illicit gatherings at the Hippodrome, attracting an eclectic mix of individuals, including non-humans. The Ghost of March exerts influence from a compound of interconnected buildings within the Undercity, guided by Matilda Manukian, a half-elf well-versed in navigating this hidden realm. The Sekbans operate with a modern military structure and diverse membership, including non-human recruits, focusing on strategic military tactics and modern weaponry, supported by training facilities and a curated library of military history.

The faction is associated with Alemdar Mustafa Pasha, a reputedly deceased commander who emphasizes unity among various groups within the Empire. The Sekbans maintain a compound in the Undercity for training and strategizing, styled as a "new army," with uniforms that resemble historical attire or consist of surplus materials. Alemdar has expressed a desire for collaboration with the Janissaries, despite past enmity, and has tasked outsiders with negotiating terms for a united front. The Sekbans actively recruit individuals skilled in elements or alchemy and are noted for their tactical demeanor and readiness for potential conflict, while Alemdar seeks to maintain the appearance of ongoing rivalry with the Janissaries to mislead others about their true intentions.

Recently, the Sekbans have taken on the role of the current guard within the city, having emerged from the former elite corps known as the Janissaries. This faction has undergone a modernization shift under the Potasha’s Line, establishing military forces that align with the current ruling authority. They are tasked with maintaining order and security in the city, demonstrating loyalty to the Potasha rather than to previous structures of power. Notable members, including Sidra al-Najjar, Jean-Zera Mongé, and Gizem Faruki, recently engaged in a search within the catacombs to locate missing Janissaries who had defected. Their descent through the Basilica Cistern led them to confront various hazards, including undead creatures, where they discovered a fresh corpse of a young janissary and subsequently dealt with animated skeletons and a Giant Skeleton, ultimately defeating these threats.

During their mission, the Sekbans interacted with Alexander, a janissary defector, who provided information about other defectors and dangers present in the catacombs. After subduing Alexander, they secured his assistance in mentoring a young individual with uncontrollable magic, contingent upon secrecy regarding his whereabouts. Following their encounters, the Sekbans collected trophies from their battles and delivered them to a bounty broker, receiving compensation for their efforts. Jean-Zera Mongé conducted research on the Sekbans' history and its connections to the Janissaries, revealing the faction's evolution and its role in the current political landscape. The Sekbans' influence is now deeply intertwined with the ongoing power dynamics within the city, particularly in relation to the Potasha and external factions.
