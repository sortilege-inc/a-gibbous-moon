Session Prep: Mira & the Living Manor

☾
Keeper's numbers. Full stat blocks for the rescue at the moss-stricken house near the Trojan digsite — Mira herself and the Living Manor that guards and re-cages her. Blocks are transcribed faithfully from the campaign source; adjust to your table.

The decrypted journal has given up its secret: Bergüzar's sister hides near the ruins of Troy, and both the palace and the Ghost are racing to reach her. Whether the party comes to save her or to kill her, the scene turns on two figures — the frightened diviner who caged herself, and the house that will not let her leave.

The scene, in brief

  • Where. A decrepit ruin near the Trojan digsite — the same manor Bergüzar walks in her dreams, now moss-stricken and half-swallowed by the woods. See the real plot for why it matters.
  • The ambush first. Before the house, the party is set upon on the approach — by Aziz Sefa's bostanji and stalkers (Ghost branch), or by the Ghost's Sekban lieutenants (Mejid branch). The survivors return for a second try once Mira is taken and the manor wakes.
  • The chase. Mira flees room to room, using her spells and the house's lair actions, pleading that she is too dangerous to be taken and that she caged herself here for safety. She is not trying to kill — she is trying not to be moved.
  • The house wakes. The instant the party seizes Mira and tries to leave, the Living Manor animates. Its priority is to recapture Mira and carry her back inside, not to slaughter the party — it grapples and drags rather than kills. Once it is defeated or escaped, the road to Constantinople is open.
  • Why she matters. Mira's living presence jams Bergüzar's divination — bring the sisters within 120 ft. and neither can cast their most potent divining magic. Kill her and Bergüzar's power runs free; that is the whole point of the race.

Aura of Foresight. Her divinatory gift is so profound that any divination spell targeting her simply fails. If another creature with The Seer ability is within 120 ft. of her, she cannot cast divination spells at all. (This is the leash on Bergüzar — the sisters cancel one another.)

The Raw Power. Whenever she casts a spell of 3rd level or higher, there is a 50% chance an effect from the Raw Power table erupts around her (roll a d8):

  1. Magic Missile (1st level) is cast, its darts scattering among all creatures within 60 ft.; no creature is struck by more than one dart.
  2. Three random creatures other than Mira within 60 ft. make a Wis save vs. her spell DC or be deafened until the end of her next turn.
  3. All creatures other than Mira within 60 ft. make a Wis save vs. her spell DC, taking 6d8 psychic damage, half on a success.
  4. A random creature within 60 ft. makes a Wis save vs. her spell DC or loses the power of speech for 1 minute. (Cannot recur for 1 hour.)
  5. A Wall of Fire is cast at random; saves use Mira's spell DC. (Cannot recur for 1 hour.)
  6. Mira casts Sense the Sinner on a random creature in range, needing no components or concentration.
  7. One random creature other than Mira within 60 ft. makes a Cha save, taking 1d6 psychic damage (half on a success); if the save fails by 7 or more, the creature ages 1d6 × 5 years (reversible by greater restoration within 24 hours). (Cannot recur for 1 hour.)
  8. Karya's Mind Wipe is cast at a random creature other than Mira. (Cannot recur for 1 hour.)

Play her as terrified, not malicious: she wants only to not be moved. Misty step, dimension door, blink, invisibility, and mirror image keep her slipping from room to room; fear, confusion, and command ("Flee!" / "Drop!") scatter her captors without killing them; dominate person and telekinesis are her panic buttons. Every 3rd-level-or-higher cast risks the Raw Power table going off among friend and foe alike — she has no more control of it than the party does.

Custom Historica spells in her list: Sense the Sinner (5th-level divination, ritual, concentration ≤10 min) — a target within 30 ft. makes a Wis save or the caster witnesses its past sins as visions, unnoticed on the first casting. Karya's Mind Wipe (7th-level enchantment) — a humanoid within 10 ft. makes a Wis save or has specific knowledge erased, or one minute of memory rewritten; recalling the tampered memory brings on violent fits. (Both surface on the Raw Power table above as well.)

Damage Threshold (5). The manor is immune to all damage unless it takes 5 or more damage from a single source, in which case it takes that damage as normal. Any hit that fails to meet the threshold is superficial and does not reduce its hit points.

Origin Point. If the manor moves more than 500 ft. from its original space, it is compelled to return.

Multiattack. The manor makes two slam attacks. It can forgo one of these attacks to make a grapple check instead.

Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 16 (3d6 + 7) bludgeoning damage.

Grapple. As an action, the manor tries to grapple a Small or larger target with one of its huge hands (contested by its Athletics +10). It can hold only two targets at once — one hand each.

Run it as an obstacle, not an executioner: its whole purpose is to pluck Mira back and carry her inside. If the party grabs her and runs, the manor grapples the carrier and drags them toward the door rather than pounding them to paste. It cannot chase past 500 ft. from the house — get Mira beyond that line and the fight is effectively won. Note the low damage threshold of 5: it shrugs off chip damage and unsilvered mundane weapons entirely, so casters and magic weapons carry the fight.
