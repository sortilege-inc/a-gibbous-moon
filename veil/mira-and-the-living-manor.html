Session Prep: Mira & the Living Manor

☾
Keeper's numbers. Full stat blocks for the rescue at the moss-stricken house near the Trojan digsite — Mira herself and the Living Manor that guards and re-cages her. Rules text is quoted verbatim from the campaign source; the surrounding presentation is the Keeper's.

The decrypted journal has given up its secret: Bergüzar's sister hides near the ruins of Troy, and both the palace and the Ghost are racing to reach her. Whether the party comes to save her or to kill her, the scene turns on two figures — the frightened diviner who caged herself, and the house that will not let her leave.

The scene, in brief

  • Where. A decrepit ruin near the Trojan digsite — the same manor Bergüzar walks in her dreams, now moss-stricken and half-swallowed by the woods. See the real plot for why it matters.
  • The ambush first. Before the house, the party is set upon on the approach — by Aziz Sefa's bostanji and stalkers (Ghost branch), or by the Ghost's Sekban lieutenants (Mejid branch). The survivors return for a second try once Mira is taken and the manor wakes.
  • The chase. Mira flees room to room, using her spells and the house's lair actions, pleading that she is too dangerous to be taken and that she caged herself here for safety. She is not trying to kill — she is trying not to be moved.
  • The house wakes. The instant the party seizes Mira and tries to leave, the Living Manor animates. Its priority is to recapture Mira and carry her back inside, not to slaughter the party — it grapples and drags rather than kills. Once it is defeated or escaped, the road to Constantinople is open.
  • Why she matters. Mira's living presence jams Bergüzar's divination — bring the sisters within 120 ft. and neither can cast their most potent divining magic. Kill her and Bergüzar's power runs free; that is the whole point of the race.

Play her as terrified, not malicious: she wants only to not be moved. Misty step, dimension door, blink, invisibility, and mirror image keep her slipping from room to room; fear, confusion, and command ("Flee!" / "Drop!") scatter her captors without killing them; dominate person and telekinesis are her panic buttons. Every 3rd-level-or-higher cast risks the Raw Power table going off among friend and foe alike — she has no more control of it than the party does.

Damage Threshold (5). The Living Manor has immunity to all damage unless it takes an amount of damage equal to or greater than its damage threshold, in which case it takes damage as normal. Any damage that fails to meet or exceed the damage threshold is considered superficial and doesn't reduce the house's hit points.

Origin Point. If it moves more than 500 ft from its original space, it is compelled to return.

Multiattack. He slams two times. He can forgo one of the attacks to make a grapple check.

Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +10 to hit. Range 10 ft., one target. Hit: 16 (3d6 + 7) bludgeoning damage.

Grapple. As an action, it can try to grapple a small or larger target with one of its huge hands. Only two targets can be grappled at the same time (one hand each).

Run it as an obstacle, not an executioner: its whole purpose is to pluck Mira back and carry her inside. If the party grabs her and runs, the manor grapples the carrier and drags them toward the door rather than pounding them to paste. It cannot chase past 500 ft. from the house — get Mira beyond that line and the fight is effectively won. Note the low damage threshold of 5: it shrugs off chip damage and unsilvered mundane weapons entirely, so casters and magic weapons carry the fight.
