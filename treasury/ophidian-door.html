Ophidian Door

The Ophidian Door is a large structure located within a partially collapsed stone chamber. It features intricate designs, including an image of Aphrodite at the top and two snake-shaped handles adorned with jeweled eyes. This door serves as a significant barrier, frozen in a ward that immobilizes those who gaze upon it, as demonstrated by Frank and Heinrich, who were found entranced before it. The door's snake motifs suggest a connection to ancient myths, particularly with the tale of Heracles.

During the exploration of the chamber, Frank and Heinrich were discovered in a luminous ward scribed in the soil, standing motionless in front of the Ophidian Door. As they regained their movement, Heinrich expressed a desire to attempt to open the door again, believing it led to ancient Troy. Heinrich insisted on proceeding despite warnings of a serpent lurking in the adjacent chamber. The door's ward was disrupted when Jean-Zera Mongé kicked dust through the glowing sigil, allowing the others to sketch the door and the room for future reference.
