Non-Government Steamer

The Non-Government Steamer is a vessel of the sea fitted with decks for cargo, for sleeping, and for the parlour, and it bears passengers of every sort. It carried Osman Hamdi Bey, who kept a private room whilst others shared berths; and its character as a vessel outside the government's writ hints at something informal, perhaps even illicit, in its business.

In the course of the voyage the steamer plays host to games of chess and to easy society amongst its passengers. The mood aboard is a lively one, quickened by a live band and by opportunity for the gaming table, and her captain is a Greek veteran of the smuggling trade — a fact that argues connexions of a clandestine kind. A crisis came upon her when a spell rebounded from a ritual circle and broke open the hull, so that the ship began to take on water; and this it was that sent the company to rouse the captain and seek help, the vessel by then in a perilous way and beginning to founder.
