Petrus Gyllius diary

Petrus Gyllius Diary: A French-language notebook attributed to the scholar-explorer Petrus Gyllius, signed "PG." It was recovered sealed in a waterproof metal box beneath a cistern. The diary contains charts, drawings, and explanations about architecture and water transference mechanisms. It mentions stairs below the cistern, records that Justinian likely disposed of magic users' bodies beneath the cistern, refers to a huge cage, notes travel with fellow lodge members, and references a "dreaded she-devil."

Chronology: Jean-Zera recognized the French carving signed "PG." The waterproof box was recovered, and Jean-Zera read the diary. The entries were studied during a rest and used alongside masonry study to date the prison complex to Justinian’s era.
