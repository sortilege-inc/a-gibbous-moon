Osman's Journal

Osman's Journal is an artifact intended for a community leader. It is a fake version, suggesting it may not contain genuine information. The journal is part of a strategy by Sidra al-Najjar, Jean-Zera Mongé, and Gizem Faruki to engage with the kavalan of Egypt, an assassin. The journal's role is significant in their plans, particularly in their interactions with the community leader and the assassin. It serves as a tool for navigating the complex political landscape related to the kavalan's activities and the potential consequences of their actions.

More recently, Osman’s Journal has been identified as a tome containing information about the location of the Padishah’s consort’s sister, a powerful diviner. This diviner's existence is said to block the consort’s full arcane potential and hinder the Padishah’s grand plan. The journal is locked both mundanely and arcane, with Osman Hamdi Bey indicating that another way exists to access its contents. He bequeaths his manor, including secret compartments and an artifact vault, to his friends. The journal is discovered hidden behind an open book held by a bust in Osman’s library. Alongside it is a sealed letter addressed to his friends, which contains a black queen chess piece with a coiled metal serpent inside. In the letter, Osman implies he is likely dead and instructs his friends to seek help from an ally to unlock the journal's secrets, emphasizing that the key to the diviner's location is included.
