Black Queen Chess Piece

The Black Queen chess piece is a small black queen made of stone, with a coiled stone serpent wrapped around it. It was found inside a folded, sealed letter addressed to the party from Osman Hamdi Bey. The letter refers to the piece as "the key to where she is kept," indicating its importance in relation to a powerful diviner whose existence blocks the consort's full arcane potential. The piece was discovered during an investigation of Osman Hamdi Bey's library, which had been selectively looted. The letter containing the Black Queen chess piece was opened carefully to preserve the seal. The contents of the letter suggest that the Black Queen is tied to a larger narrative involving the Padishah's consort and the potential unlocking of arcane secrets.

While carried by the party, the Black Queen chess piece vibrated near a large Medusa statue. The serpent detached from the piece, slithered toward the statue, entered a keyhole-like opening, and activated an unlocking mechanism. This caused the statue to rotate outward, two pillars to collapse, the cistern water to drain, and a staircase to appear behind the statue.
