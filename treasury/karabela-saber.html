karabela Saber

The karabela Saber is a Polish saber displayed in pristine condition within a shrine dedicated to John III Sobieski, King of Poland and victor of the Battle of Vienna. It is noted for its historical value and is a short sword with magical properties, specifically a +1 enhancement, making it not only a relic of the past but also a desirable item for collectors. The saber was retrieved after breaking the glass case, alongside a Malkoç Family Banner and a treatise authored by Radu the Handsome.

The saber was discovered among other family heirlooms in a cabinet room, with no immediate magical effects or curses identified upon handling it. However, its intrinsic value has been recognized beyond mere history; Jean-Zera Mongé included a sketch of the karabela Saber in a letter to Giannis, emphasizing its significance in their treasure hunting endeavors. The sword was part of a hoard recovered by a party, which also contained other magical items and artifacts related to Church history and the lineage of Vlad and Radu’s father.

Current developments reveal that the saber's connection to these historical figures and events enhances its allure, intertwining it with the legacies of both the Polish crown and the tumultuous history of Eastern Europe.
