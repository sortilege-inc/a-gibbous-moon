Sword Of Osman

The Sword of Osman is a ceremonial weapon presented during the coronation of Crown Shazada Majid. It is associated with the rituals of becoming the potasha and symbolizes authority and leadership. The sword is presented to Majid at the steps of the Hagia Sophia, where he accepts it in front of an assembled crowd. Guards distribute small bags of coins to the attendees, leading to some audience members expressing discontent by throwing the coins back, declaring that the gold belongs to the janissaries.

The presence of the Sword of Osman evokes a mixed reaction among the crowd, reflecting historical tensions surrounding the janissaries, who were officially disbanded but remain a topic of discussion. The distribution of coins and subsequent protests reveal underlying unrest related to the legacy of the janissaries and their connection to the Sword of Osman. The ceremony's atmosphere is festive yet charged with historical significance, as the sword symbolizes both a new beginning and the weight of past conflicts.
